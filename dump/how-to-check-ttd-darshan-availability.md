Content:You're planning your Tirumala trip and want to book the train — but first you go to check when darshan slots are even available. The TTD site opens, a few options appear, you can't tell where to click, and in frustration you close the tab. That experience is incredibly common.

The TTD website looks confusing at first, but once you understand the process, checking dates takes about two minutes. The bigger issue is that most people check the wrong way — they expect a simple "45-day rolling window," when in 2026 TTD actually releases each month's quota in one batch on an announced date. Knowing that is half the battle.

This guide shows you exactly how to check TTD darshan dates in 2026 — on the website and the official app — how to read the calendar colours, when the quota actually opens, and how to catch cancellation slots. It's written for first-timers who just want a clear answer.

A note from experience: I've booked TTD darshan many times, and the people who get the good dates aren't lucky — they simply knew the release date for their travel month and were logged in and ready when it opened.
<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — Checking TTD darshan dates:

 	<li>🗓️ Quotas are released monthly, on an announced date (often around the 21st–24th), ~2–3 months ahead — not a rolling 45-day window.</li>
 	<li>🟢 In the calendar, green/active = slots available; grey = booked or not yet released.</li>
 	<li>⚡ Popular dates can vanish within minutes — be logged in and ready at release.</li>
 	<li>🔁 Missed it? Watch for cancellation slots, which reopen randomly.</li>
 	<li>📰 Daily darshan status/figures are posted on news.tirumala.org.</li>

<h2>How to check dates on the TTD website</h2>
<ol>
 	<li>Open the official portal: <strong>ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in</strong> (also reachable as tirupatibalaji.ap.gov.in).</li>
 	<li>Go to the darshan/booking section and choose <strong>Special Entry Darshan</strong>.</li>
 	<li>Log in (register first if you're new — it's free).</li>
 	<li>A calendar appears — available dates show as green/active.</li>
 	<li>Booked-out or unreleased dates appear grey/disabled.</li>
 	<li>Click an available date to see the time slots.</li>
</ol>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> The login page is heavily loaded right when a month's window opens. Off-peak hours (late night or early morning) are faster for routine checking. If the site keeps crashing, the <a href="/ttd-booking-site-crash-tips/">booking-site crash tips</a> help.

<h2>Checking on the TTD Govinda App</h2>
The official app is often easier than the website, especially on slow internet. Download the <strong>TTD Govinda App</strong> (the official one), open the darshan booking section, log in, choose Special Entry Darshan, and view the calendar.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Only use the official app — there are fake "TTD" apps on the stores. Check the publisher is Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams. Booking through a fake app or third-party site is a fraud risk. More on the app in the <a href="/ttd-mobile-app-guide/">TTD app guide</a>.

<h2>💡 Gap: when slots actually open (the real release model)</h2>
This is what most guides get wrong. TTD doesn't simply roll out "45 days at a time." It releases each month's darshan quota in a batch, on a pre-announced date — commonly around the <strong>21st–24th of the month</strong>, for travel roughly two to three months later. For example, June dates were announced to open in March.

What this means for you:
<ul>
 	<li>Find the release date for your <em>travel month</em> (TTD announces it on news.tirumala.org and the portal).</li>
 	<li>Popular dates — weekends and festivals — are first-come and can vanish within about 5 minutes.</li>
 	<li>Weekday and off-season dates stay available longer.</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Set a reminder for your month's release date and be logged in just before the release time. The exact schedule shifts, so confirm it on <a href="https://www.tirumala.org/" rel="nofollow">tirumala.org</a>. To choose low-crowd dates, use the <a href="/tirumala-darshan-best-time-month-by-month-month-by-month/">best time guide</a> and the <a href="/ttd-calendar-2026/">TTD 2026 calendar</a>.
<h2>💡 Gap: checking live availability &amp; cancellation slots</h2>
Beyond the booking calendar, there are two underused ways to stay ahead:
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Daily status:</strong> TTD posts daily darshan figures and the current free-darshan waiting time on news.tirumala.org — useful for gauging how heavy your travel dates are. See also the <a href="/ttd-darshan-news-today/">TTD darshan news</a>.</li>
 	<li><strong>Cancellation slots:</strong> If your date is sold out, don't give up — cancelled slots return to the calendar, most often in the 7–10 days before the travel date. Check the portal 2–3 times a day, and book the instant a slot appears, because it can disappear on the next refresh.</li>
</ul>
There's no official alert system for cancellations, so it's manual — but persistent checking genuinely works.
<h2>Sarva Darshan dates — how to check</h2>
Free Sarva Darshan doesn't have an advance-date calendar like the paid darshan — it runs on the SSD token system. You collect a free token at Tirupati counters (often for next-day darshan), so there's no months-ahead booking to "check." The process is in the <a href="/ttd-sarva-darshan-ticket-booking/">Sarva Darshan booking guide</a>, and token types in the <a href="/tirumala-darshan-token-system-2026-epass-vs-physical-token-vs-online-slot-2026-epass-vs-physical-token-vs-online-slot/">token system guide</a>.

<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — checking darshan dates</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Expecting a rolling 45-day window:</strong> It's a monthly batch release on an announced date — find your travel month's release date.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Trying to view the calendar without logging in:</strong> The slot calendar shows only after login — create a free account first.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Reading grey as "not released yet":</strong> It can also mean sold out — cross-check the schedule.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Seeing a slot and booking "later":</strong> Available slots can vanish in minutes — book the moment you see one.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Using fake apps/third-party sites:</strong> Only the official portal and TTD Govinda App are safe.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking train before the darshan slot:</strong> Confirm darshan first, then train and hotel.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Checking only 1–2 weeks before travel:</strong> Good dates are long gone by then — check at the release date.</li>
</ul>
