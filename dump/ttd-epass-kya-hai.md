Content:Picture this: you booked the ₹300 SED ticket, payment went through, the confirmation SMS arrived. But on darshan day you reach the gate and realise you never downloaded the ePass. There's no network on your phone, the TTD site won't load, and now it's a frantic 45 minutes at the gate with the whole family standing around stressed.

That exact situation is what this guide prevents. The TTD ePass is your digital entry ticket — and using it correctly is simple, once you know how. The mistakes happen only from not preparing it in advance.

This article covers everything: what the ePass is, how to download it, what it contains, how to show it at the gate, and how to troubleshoot when it goes missing at the worst moment.

I have been to Tirumala many times over the years — and yes, the missing-ePass scramble happened to me once too. Since then I download the ePass the moment booking is done, save it on the phone, and keep a printout in my pocket. This guide comes from that practical experience. Confirm any live details on the official site, since rules can change.
<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — TTD ePass:
<ul>
 	<li>ePass = a digital entry ticket (PDF) generated after TTD online booking.</li>
 	<li>It contains a QR code, pilgrim name, slot time and ID number.</li>
 	<li>At the gate, show both the ePass and the matching original ID proof.</li>
 	<li>A PDF or a clear screenshot on the phone is accepted.</li>
 	<li>A printout is highly recommended — a backup for network issues.</li>
 	<li>For SED/Seva tickets, entry is impossible without the ePass.</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<h2>How to download the ePass — step by step</h2>
[caption id="attachment_1396" align="alignnone" width="1024"]<img class="wp-image-1396 size-large" src="https://tirumalatirupatiguide.in/wp-content/uploads/2026/06/ttd-epass-download-save-print-step-by-step-guide-1024x576.webp" alt="TTD ePass की पूरी process — online booking से download, phone में save, printout लेना, और तिरुमला gate पर QR scan तक के 4 steps का infographic illustration" width="1024" height="576" /> Booking → Download → Save + Print → Gate Entry। TTD ePass की ये 4 steps याद रखो — Tirumala में network नहीं मिलेगा, इसलिए पहले से तैयार रहो।[/caption]

After your booking is complete, downloading the ePass is straightforward.
<h3>Step 1: Log in to the TTD portal</h3>
Go to the official TTD portal (tirupatibalaji.ap.gov.in / tirumala.org) and log in with the registered mobile/email — the same credentials you used to book.
<h3>Step 2: Open "My Bookings" / "Booking History"</h3>
The dashboard shows all your upcoming bookings. Click the one whose ePass you need.
<h3>Step 3: Click "Download ePass" / "Print"</h3>
On the booking details page, a "Download ePass" button generates the PDF. Some cases also show a "Print" option that opens the print dialogue directly.
<h3>Step 4: Save on the phone + take a printout</h3>
Save the PDF in your phone's Files/Downloads folder. And take a physical printout too — A4, black &amp; white is fine.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> After downloading, keep the ePass accessible offline on your phone. Network on Tirumala is very patchy, especially in the queue area. If the PDF is saved on the phone, you won't need the internet at all.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The ePass is available immediately after the booking is confirmed — there's no "you'll get it after 24 hours." Payment successful = ePass ready.
<h2>What the ePass contains</h2>
Your ePass PDF will carry:
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Pilgrim name(s):</strong> everyone included in the booking</li>
 	<li><strong>Booking reference number:</strong> a unique ID — useful for any complaint</li>
 	<li><strong>Darshan type:</strong> SED ₹300 / Seva name / SSD token</li>
 	<li><strong>Slot date &amp; time:</strong> the exact date and time window</li>
 	<li><strong>QR code:</strong> for scanning at the gate — the most important element</li>
 	<li><strong>ID proof number:</strong> the ID given at booking (Aadhaar/Passport/Voter ID)</li>
 	<li><strong>Number of persons:</strong> the total count</li>
 	<li><strong>Reporting time:</strong> when to reach, ahead of the slot</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The ID number printed on the ePass is the original ID you must show at the gate. If you booked with Aadhaar, carrying that Aadhaar card is mandatory. A mismatch means entry denied.
<h2>How to use the ePass at the entry gate</h2>
<h3>Reach by the reporting time</h3>
The ePass shows a reporting time — usually 1–2 hours before the slot. Reach the designated gate at this time.
<h3>Verification at the gate</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Security staff scan the ePass QR code</li>
 	<li>Your original ID proof is checked (photo match + number match)</li>
 	<li>Each member's ID is checked individually</li>
 	<li>Successful verification = entry allowed</li>
</ul>
<h3>💡 Gap — phone or printout: which to show?</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>✅ A PDF opened on the phone screen works</li>
 	<li>✅ A screenshot is accepted too (the QR code must be clear)</li>
 	<li>✅ An A4 printout — the most reliable option</li>
 	<li>❌ A blurry screenshot or low brightness — the QR scan can fail</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Keep phone brightness at maximum when showing the ePass. Early-morning slots (4–6 AM) are dark, and the scanner struggles to read a QR code off a dim screen. A printout is the safest bet.
<h2>💡 Gap — ePass not showing: troubleshooting</h2>
<h3>Problem 1: Booking done, but no download option</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Check the payment status — sometimes payment stays pending</li>
 	<li>Money debited but not reflecting on TTD: wait 30 minutes, then re-login</li>
 	<li>Still not showing after 2 hours: contact the official TTD support channel listed on tirumala.org</li>
</ul>
<h3>Problem 2: ePass downloaded but the QR code is blank</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Download the PDF again — it may be a browser cache issue</li>
 	<li>Try a different device (laptop/tablet)</li>
 	<li>Download from the official TTD app — the app version often renders better quality</li>
</ul>
<h3>Problem 3: Can't find the ePass on darshan day</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Search your email for "TTD" or "ePass" — the confirmation email has the PDF attached</li>
 	<li>Open the TTD app — it'll be under "My Bookings"</li>
 	<li>Last resort: the gate help desk — give the booking reference number, and they can verify and allow entry (but it takes time)</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> "The help desk will sort it out" is a backup plan, not the primary one. The help desk has a queue and the process can take 20–30 minutes. Keeping the ePass ready in advance is the smart move.
<h2>ePass vs physical ticket — what's the difference?</h2>
<ul>
 	<li><strong>ePass (online):</strong> PDF format, phone or printout both valid, QR-code verification</li>
 	<li><strong>Physical ticket (offline counter):</strong> issued at the TTD counters in Tirupati, a paper ticket, barcode-based</li>
 	<li>Both are equally valid — the entry process is the same</li>
 	<li>Online booking = ePass | Offline booking = physical ticket</li>
</ul>
If you did an <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">online SED booking</a>, you'll get an ePass. If you bought a ticket at a Tirupati counter, you'll have a physical ticket.
<h2>ePass rules to know — 2026</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>✅ Up to a set maximum of persons per ePass (family booking limit — generally up to 6)</li>
 	<li>✅ Infants (under 1 year): need not be on the ePass, but carry a birth certificate</li>
 	<li>✅ Children 1–12 years: their name must be added to the ePass</li>
 	<li>❌ The ePass is non-transferable — no name change</li>
 	<li>❌ An expired ePass (slot date passed) has no value</li>
 	<li>✅ Re-downloading: the same booking can be downloaded multiple times (if lost, download again)</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If your family has more than the per-booking limit, you'll need two separate bookings — two ePasses, two primary bookers. If you get the same slot time, you can still enter together.
<h2>ePass via the TTD app — the faster way</h2>
Managing the ePass through the official TTD Govinda app is more convenient than the website:
<ul>
 	<li>"My Bookings" shows directly in the app</li>
 	<li>The ePass auto-saves with a notification</li>
 	<li>Previously downloaded ePasses stay accessible in offline mode</li>
 	<li>The QR code renders in better quality in the app</li>
</ul>
🌅 <strong>Pro Tip:</strong> Save the ePass in the app the moment you book. A day before travel, open it once to confirm all the details are correct.
<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not downloading the ePass after booking:</strong> "I'll do it later" turns into forgetting. On darshan day there's no network on Tirumala, and panic starts. Download and save immediately after booking.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>A screenshot with the QR code cropped:</strong> keep a full-page screenshot or the PDF. A cropped or blurry QR code won't scan.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Carrying a different ID than the one in the booking:</strong> if the ePass shows Aadhaar and you carry a driving licence, entry is denied. Carry the same ID you gave at booking.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>The primary booker being absent at the gate:</strong> "I'll come later, you go in" doesn't work. The primary booker's physical presence and ID are mandatory.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Packing the printout in your luggage:</strong> bags go into the locker on Tirumala. Keep the ePass in your pocket, not the bag. See the <a href="/tirumala-mobile-locker-guide/">locker guide</a> — if the bag goes in the locker, the ePass goes with it!</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Showing an old/expired ePass:</strong> an old ePass in the phone gallery causes confusion. Identify the current booking's ePass and save it separately — put the date in the filename.</li>
</ul>
