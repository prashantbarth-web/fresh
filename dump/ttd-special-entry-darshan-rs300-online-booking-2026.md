<h2>What is TTD Special Entry Darshan?</h2>
Tirumala has many darshan systems. The TTD Special Entry Darshan — which pilgrims call the ₹300 darshan — is the one where you book a fixed time slot in advance. Compared with the free Sarva Darshan queue, the wait is far shorter.

It isn't a "walk straight in" VIP darshan — you'll still stand in a line — but instead of the free queue's 18–24 hours, this is usually done in about 2–4 hours. It suits:
<ul>
 	<li>First-timers who want a planned, predictable trip</li>
 	<li>Families with elderly parents or small children</li>
 	<li>Anyone on a one- or two-day schedule who can't risk a 20-hour wait</li>
</ul>
The ₹300 applies per person, child or adult, except infants under five who go free and aren't counted. To see how this fits among all the options, read <a href="/types-of-tirumala-darshan/">types of Tirumala darshan</a> and the honest <a href="/tirumala-darshan-waiting-time-2026-2026/">waiting time guide</a>.

<h2>💡 Gap: when the quota actually opens now (most guides get this wrong)</h2>
Older articles tell you "slots release on the 1st of every month at midnight." That's outdated, and it's why people miss out.

What TTD actually does in 2026: the ₹300 quota for a given month is released on a specific, pre-announced date — generally at <strong>10 AM</strong> — typically around two to three months in advance. For example, the September quota was announced for release on a June date at 10 AM. It's published alongside the month's other quotas (accommodation, sevas).

So the real strategy is:
<ul>
 	<li>Watch tirumala.org / TTD news for the release date and time of your travel month — don't assume the 1st.</li>
 	<li>Be logged in and ready a few minutes before the announced time (usually 10 AM).</li>
 	<li>Popular dates can sell out within minutes — have everything pre-filled.</li>
</ul>
You can track release dates with the <a href="/how-to-check-ttd-darshan-availability/">how to check darshan dates guide</a>, and confirm current timings on <a href="https://www.tirumala.org/" rel="nofollow">tirumala.org</a>.

<h2>TTD online booking — step by step</h2>
<ol>
 	<li><strong>Open the official portal:</strong> ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in (linked from tirumala.org). Don't use lookalike sites that appear in Google ads.</li>
 	<li><strong>Register / log in:</strong> New users register with mobile number → OTP → set a password. The <a href="/ttd-mobile-app-guide/">TTD app</a> works too and is often faster.</li>
 	<li><strong>Go to Special Entry Darshan:</strong> Find the darshan booking section in the dashboard.</li>
 	<li><strong>Pick date &amp; time slot:</strong> Available dates show as bookable; choose your slot (an early-morning slot leaves the rest of the day free).</li>
 	<li><strong>Add members:</strong> Name (as per Aadhaar), age, gender, ID type and number — up to 6 per booking. Double-check before submitting.</li>
 	<li><strong>Pay:</strong> ₹300 per person (e.g., 4 members = ₹1,200). UPI is quickest. Screenshot the confirmation.</li>
 	<li><strong>Download &amp; print the ticket:</strong> Save the PDF and keep a printout — it shows your reference number, slot, gate and reporting time.</li>
</ol>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> The site can crash under the rush at release time. Don't hammer refresh — let the page load, wait a couple of minutes, then retry. More tips are in the <a href="/ttd-booking-site-crash-tips/">TTD booking site crash guide</a>.
<h2>💡 Gap: it's online-only now — and beware brokers</h2>
The old guides list offline ₹300 counters at Alipiri, the CRO and Tirumala. In 2026 that's no longer reliable — the Special Entry Darshan is effectively online-only, with no dependable offline sale, especially in peak season.

That matters because it kills a popular myth: that you can "just buy a ₹300 ticket at a counter when you reach." You usually can't. And it makes you a target for touts.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> TTD repeatedly warns devotees not to book darshan through brokers or middlemen and runs a complaint line (toll-free 155257). Anyone promising a "guaranteed ₹300 ticket" for extra cash is a scam — fake tickets get rejected at the gate. Book only on the official portal. If you can't get a slot, the free <a href="/ttd-sarva-darshan-ticket-booking/">SSD Sarva Darshan token</a> is always available.
<h2>Family booking &amp; children</h2>
One TTD account allows up to 6 members per booking. For 7 or more, create a second account and book the same date and slot — you'll get separate tickets but join the queue together. For children: under-5 infants are free and not counted; children 5 and above need a ₹300 ticket and must be listed on the form.

<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Waiting for the "1st of the month":</strong> The quota releases on an announced date (usually 10 AM), 2–3 months ahead. Watch tirumala.org, not the calendar's 1st.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Name mismatch with Aadhaar:</strong> Enter names exactly as on the ID, or risk being turned away at the gate.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Expecting an offline counter ticket:</strong> It's online-only now. Book on the portal before you travel.</li>
