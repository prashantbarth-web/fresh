<h2>What is Special Entry Darshan ₹300?</h2>
Special Entry Darshan (SED), often just called the "₹300 darshan," is TTD's paid darshan option: you pay ₹300 per person, book a fixed time slot in advance online, and enter through a separate, shorter queue instead of the long free Sarva Darshan line. The darshan inside the sanctum is exactly the same — only the waiting time is shorter.

So the ₹300 is essentially a processing fee for a faster, planned queue. You can book it from home on your phone or laptop, choose your date and slot, and skip standing for many hours. To see where it sits among all options, read <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">types of Tirumala darshan</a>.
<h2>💡 Gap: it's not VIP or "closer" darshan</h2>
This is the misconception to drop. The ₹300 does <strong>not</strong> get you nearer the deity, a longer look, or any special treatment inside the temple. Everyone — free or paid — gets the same 30–60 seconds before Lord Venkateswara, and security keeps the line moving for all alike.

What you're paying for is purely a shorter, more predictable wait and the ability to plan your trip around a fixed slot. It's also not the same as the genuinely premium tiers like SRIVANI break darshan — for that comparison, see the <a href="/tirumala-privilege-darshan-guide/">VIP / privilege darshan guide</a>. Treat SED as a paid fast-track queue, nothing more — and that's exactly why it's such good value.

<h2>What your ₹300 includes (and doesn't)</h2>
<strong>You get:</strong> a fixed date and time slot, entry through a separate shorter SED queue, a much smaller wait than free darshan, and the ability to plan travel and hotel around a known slot.

<strong>You don't get:</strong> any VIP or close-up darshan, guaranteed zero wait (there's still a 2–4 hour line), or laddu prasadam — that's bought separately at the laddu counter (see the <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">laddu prasadam guide</a>).
<h2>💡 Gap: the 2026 booking reality</h2>
A lot of older guides say "book 45 days in advance" and "offline counters also sell it." Both are outdated in 2026:
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Online only:</strong> SED is sold through the official TTD portal/app — there's no reliable offline counter sale anymore.</li>
 	<li><strong>Released on an announced date:</strong> The monthly quota opens on a pre-announced date (often around 10 AM), typically a couple of months ahead — not a rolling "45-day" window. Popular dates sell out within minutes.</li>
 	<li><strong>Slot-strict:</strong> Your time slot is enforced — reach the SED counter well before it.</li>
 	<li><strong>Availability varies:</strong> The ₹300 structure has changed over 2024–2026, so it isn't always open; confirm on the official site.</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Verify the current release date and availability on <a href="https://www.tirumala.org/" rel="nofollow">tirumala.org</a>. The full step-by-step is in the <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">SED online booking guide</a>.
<h2>How the time slot works</h2>
You're given a window like "6:00 AM–8:00 AM." Reach the SED counter at least 2 hours before, where your booking and ID are verified before you join the queue. Arriving late can forfeit your slot with no refund, and the slot date can't be changed — so choose a slot that comfortably fits your train arrival and hotel check-in.
<h2>Who should choose SED vs free darshan</h2>
<strong>Choose SED if:</strong> you're with elderly parents or small children, you have only a day, you're going in peak season (when free darshan hits 18–24 hours), or you simply can't manage a long queue.

<strong>Choose free Sarva Darshan if:</strong> you have 2–3 flexible days, you're on a tight budget, you're going in low season (July–August) or on a Monday/Tuesday when the free wait is shorter, or SED slots aren't available for your dates. The <a href="/tirumala-darshan-best-time/">best time guide</a> helps you pick low-crowd dates.
<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — about SED</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Thinking it's VIP darshan:</strong> It's just a paid fast-track queue — same darshan inside for everyone.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Reaching exactly at slot time:</strong> Get there ~2 hours early for verification.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking travel before the slot:</strong> Confirm the SED slot first, then book train and hotel.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Expecting an offline ticket:</strong> It's online-only now — don't plan to buy one on arrival.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Trying for weekend slots last-minute:</strong> Saturday–Sunday slots go fast; prefer weekdays.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Ignoring the dress code:</strong> It applies to SED too — read the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">dress code rules</a>.</li>
