Which Mumbai station? Train, flight, or drive? For pilgrims from Maharashtra, these are the exact questions that stall the planning.

The good news: Mumbai is genuinely closer to Tirupati than the North Indian cities, so the journey is shorter and there are real overnight train options. The catch is the detail — the right Mumbai station to leave from, and the fact that some trains arrive at Renigunta rather than Tirupati city. Get those two things right and the trip is easy.

This guide is written for Mumbai and wider Maharashtra pilgrims. It lays out the train options honestly, compares flights, explains when the road trip actually makes sense, and gives the practical advice that matters for a family journey.

I have travelled from the North and West to Tirumala several times over the years, including with Mumbai-based family, and they almost always pick the overnight train. So treat the timings and fares below as a planning baseline, then confirm the live details on IRCTC before booking — schedules shift.

<h2>Mumbai to Tirupati — distance and overview</h2>
Mumbai to Tirupati is roughly 1,000–1,200 km — noticeably less than from UP or Bihar. That is why the train ride is around 18–23 hours here, against the 32–40 hours from the North. An evening departure and a next-day arrival makes the overnight train very workable.

Mumbai has several rail terminals — Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Terminus (CSTM), Mumbai Central (BCT), Lokmanya Tilak Terminus (LTT) and Dadar. For trains heading toward Tirupati, LTT carries the most useful services.
<h2>Option 1 — Train: the most practical choice</h2>
<h3>The honest truth about "direct" trains</h3>
A guaranteed daily, no-change train straight into Tirupati (TPTY) is limited from Mumbai. Many services run toward Chennai and stop at Renigunta Junction (RU) — about 10 km from Tirupati — from where you take an auto, cab or local connection. So the smart move is to search both stations on IRCTC and check whether your train ends at Tirupati or Renigunta.

💡 <strong>Gap — the station detail older guides get wrong:</strong> Some blogs list a fixed "Mumbai–Tirupati Express" with a confident train number, but those numbers often belong to a different route entirely. Availability changes with the timetable, and a lot of "direct" results actually arrive at Renigunta. Do not lock your plan to one number from a blog — open IRCTC, search <strong>LTT → TPTY</strong> and <strong>CSTM → TPTY</strong> for your exact date, and read whether it terminates at TPTY or RU.

<h3>Booking tips on IRCTC</h3>
<ol>
 	<li>Open the IRCTC website or Rail Connect app.</li>
 	<li>From: LTT or CSTM → To: <strong>TPTY</strong> (also check <strong>RU</strong> / Renigunta).</li>
 	<li>General booking opens 60 days ahead — try on the opening day for peak dates.</li>
 	<li>In peak season (October–January, Diwali, school holidays) confirmed berths go fast.</li>
 	<li>Tatkal opens one day before travel, with extra charges.</li>

<h3>From Tirupati Airport onward</h3>
Tirupati Airport (Renigunta) is about 15 km from Tirupati city, and Tirumala is roughly 22 km further up the ghat road. A prepaid taxi to the city runs ~₹400–600; you can also take a cab straight up to Tirumala for around ₹1,500–2,000.

<h2>Darshan booking — do this right after booking travel</h2>
As soon as your travel is booked, sort out your darshan ticket.
<ul>
 	<li>The ₹300 Special Entry Darshan is online-only — released as a monthly quota on a pre-announced date (generally a couple of months ahead), not a fixed 45-day window.</li>
 	<li>Popular dates sell out within minutes, so be ready when the month's quota opens.</li>
 	<li>Each ticket is non-transferable — the named person must show the matching original ID.</li>
 	<li>No ₹300 slot? Free Sarva Darshan is the backup — now the SSD token system, with tokens collected at Tirupati counters.</li>
</ul>
Step-by-step: <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">TTD ₹300 Special Entry Darshan online booking guide</a>. To compare every option, read <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">types of Tirumala darshan</a>.
<h2>❌ Common mistakes — Mumbai to Tirupati</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Going to the wrong Mumbai station:</strong> CSTM, BCT, LTT and Dadar are all different. Confirm the exact departure terminal on IRCTC — reaching the wrong one means missing the train.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not checking for a Renigunta arrival:</strong> some trains end at Renigunta (RU), 10 km from Tirupati. Know this in advance.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Underestimating the time:</strong> at 18–23 hours, a same-day return is impossible. Plan a minimum of 3 days.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Driving the ghat road at night:</strong> reach Tirupati before evening and avoid the ghat section after dark.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Trying to drive up to Tirumala:</strong> private vehicles are restricted — park at Alipiri and take the TTD bus.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Skipping the darshan ticket:</strong> book the ₹300 slot when the month's quota opens, so you are not stuck in a long free-line wait after a 20-hour journey.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not packing traditional clothes:</strong> the Tirumala dress code is strict — read the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">Tirumala dress code rules</a> before you pack.</li>
</ul>
