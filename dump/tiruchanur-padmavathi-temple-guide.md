<h2>Why is the Padmavathi temple important?</h2>
In Hindu tradition, the story of Lord Venkateswara and Goddess Padmavathi is deeply connected. By belief, Padmavathi resides at Tiruchanur — a small town close to Tirupati.

The conviction is that the darshan of Lord Venkateswara is complete only when one also has the darshan of Padmavathi Devi. That is why most South Indian pilgrims pair Tiruchanur with Tirumala — it is treated almost like a package.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Tirumala's crowd and energy are intense, even overwhelming. Tiruchanur is comparatively quiet. Arriving here after Tirumala brings a noticeably different calm.

<h2>About the temple — history and architecture</h2>
The Tiruchanur Padmavathi temple is built in the Dravidian style — a large gopuram (entrance tower), the main sanctum and various mandapams. It is maintained by TTD.

The main deity, Goddess Padmavathi, is seated on a lotus, four-armed and adorned in gold ornaments. The atmosphere is gentle and peaceful — quite different from Tirumala's powerful intensity.

Within the complex you will find:
<ul>
 	<li>The main sanctum — Padmavathi Devi</li>
 	<li>A Sri Venkateswara shrine — darshan is offered here too</li>
 	<li>Swami Pushkarini — a sacred tank near the temple</li>
 	<li>A TTD prasadam counter — laddu and other prasadam</li>
</ul>

<h2>How to reach Tiruchanur from Tirupati</h2>
<h3>Option 1: APSRTC bus (cheapest)</h3>
Regular buses run from the Tirupati Central Bus Stand to Tiruchanur.
<ul>
 	<li>Frequency: every 15–20 minutes</li>
 	<li>Ticket: ₹15–₹20</li>
 	<li>Journey time: 20–25 minutes</li>
 	<li>Ask the conductor to confirm the Tirupati–Tiruchanur route</li>
</ul>
<h3>Option 2: Auto-rickshaw</h3>
From the Tirupati railway station or town.
<ul>
 	<li>Fare: ₹80–₹120 one way (metered or negotiated)</li>
 	<li>Journey time: 15–20 minutes</li>
 	<li>Advantage: door-to-door, flexible timing</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Return autos are easy to find outside the temple — there is no prepaid stand, but shared autos to Tirupati are always available.
<h3>Option 3: TTD free bus</h3>
TTD runs some Tirupati–Tiruchanur free buses at specific times, convenient for pilgrims coming down from Tirumala. Check availability at the TTD counter.
<h3>Option 4: Taxi / cab</h3>
Ola/Uber operate in Tirupati.
<ul>
 	<li>Fare: ₹150–₹250 one way</li>
 	<li>Best for: families, seniors, or when you have luggage</li>
</ul>
For all the local options, see the <a href="/tirupati-town-guide/">Tirupati town guide</a>.
<h2>💡 Gap — where does Tiruchanur fit in your Tirumala trip?</h2>
This is the most practical question — in a 2-day plan, when do you do Tiruchanur?
<h3>Option A — Day 1 afternoon (before going up to Tirumala)</h3>
Reach Tirupati → visit Tiruchanur (2–4 PM) → back to Tirupati → evening bus up to Tirumala.
<em>Advantage:</em> you are fresh and have energy, and you receive Padmavathi's blessing before Venkateswara — which matches tradition.
<h3>Option B — Day 2 on the way back (after coming down from Tirumala)</h3>
Tirumala darshan → back to Tirupati → Tiruchanur → station.
<em>Advantage:</em> your Tirumala darshan is done, so you visit Tiruchanur without time pressure. This is the sequence most pilgrims follow.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> If your train is in the afternoon, do not try to dash to Tiruchanur the moment you come down from Tirumala. Go to the station first, store your luggage, and then visit Tiruchanur — doing it in reverse risks missing the train. See where it fits in the <a href="/tirupati-2-day-itinerary/">complete 2-day Tirupati itinerary</a>, or for a tight schedule, the <a href="/tirupati-1-day-darshan/">1-day darshan plan</a>.
<h2>Dress code — what to wear</h2>
The dress code at Tiruchanur is not as strict as Tirumala, but traditional clothing is expected.
<ul>
 	<li>✅ Men: dhoti / kurta-pyjama / formal trousers and shirt</li>
 	<li>✅ Women: saree / salwar suit / churidar</li>
 	<li>❌ Shorts, sleeveless and jeans — avoid (TTD temple premises)</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If you have come straight from Tirumala and were already in a dhoti, the same outfit works here — no need for a change. The full guidance is in the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules-2026-2026/">Tirumala dress code guide</a>.
<h2>Prasadam and offerings</h2>
The Tiruchanur temple has a TTD prasadam counter offering:
<ul>
 	<li>Laddu — the same TTD Tirumala recipe (around ₹50 for the small one)</li>
 	<li>Vada mala — garlands for offering</li>
 	<li>Coconut and flowers — available at the counter</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> There is no need to buy prasadam from shops outside — authentic prasadam is available at the TTD counter inside. The full picture is in the <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">Tirumala laddu prasad guide</a>.

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Visiting Tirumala but missing Tiruchanur:</strong> the most common one. Once you're in Tirupati, Tiruchanur is just 5 km away — a comfortable half-day visit. Skipping it leads to regret later.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Arriving during the midday break (12–2 PM):</strong> the temple is closed and you'll have to come back. Check the timing first.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>A last-minute Tiruchanur dash on your train day:</strong> tired, an afternoon train, and a sudden plan — luggage, timing and traffic all become a risk. Don't squeeze it in before departure without a comfortable buffer.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Buying prasadam from outside shops:</strong> overpriced and unnecessary — the same prasadam is at the TTD counter inside.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Going at the 5–8 PM peak without a plan:</strong> the busiest slot, with 2-hour waits on weekends. A morning visit is always better.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Ignoring the dress code:</strong> shorts or sleeveless won't get you in. Coming straight from Tirumala means your dress is already sorted.</li>
</ul>
