Content:Let me be honest — the first time I tried the TTD app, I expected the usual government-app experience: slow, confusing, prone to crashing. Pleasant surprise: it actually works. Since around 2024, TTD has improved the app a lot, and now everything from darshan tickets to accommodation can be done from the phone.

I have been to Tirumala several times over the years — the first few trips I booked through the website, and the last couple via the app. Honestly, the app experience is better than the website, especially for features like ePass download and slot reminders.

But the app has limitations too, and I'll be upfront about them in this article. Not everything is perfect.

This guide covers it all — the official download (and how to avoid the fakes), setup, booking, the standout features, an honest list of limitations, and when to use the app versus the website. Confirm any live details on the official site, since the app updates often.
<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — TTD Mobile App:
<ul>
 	<li>App name: "TTD" by developer "Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams" — on Android (Play Store) and iOS (App Store).</li>
 	<li>Free download — no subscription.</li>
 	<li>Features: SED booking, Seva booking, Accommodation, ePass download, darshan slot status.</li>
 	<li>Registration: mobile number + OTP — the same credentials as the website.</li>
 	<li>App size: ~50 MB (Android) | ~80 MB (iOS).</li>
 	<li>Minimum: Android 6.0+ | iOS 13+.</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<h2>How to download the TTD app</h2>
<h3>Android (Google Play Store)</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Open the Play Store</li>
 	<li>Search: "TTD" or "Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams"</li>
 	<li>Verify the developer name: <strong>Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams</strong> — that is the official app</li>
 	<li>Tap "Install" — about 50 MB</li>
 	<li>Once installed, open the app</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Searching "TTD" on the Play Store throws up 4–5 apps, some of them unofficial third-party ones. Download only the one by the developer "Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams." The others can leak personal data.
<h3>iPhone (App Store)</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Open the App Store</li>
 	<li>Search: "TTD"</li>
 	<li>Select the official app (the temple-logo icon)</li>
 	<li>Tap "Get" and confirm with Face ID/Touch ID</li>
 	<li>About 80 MB — ready to use</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Check phone storage before downloading — about 500 MB free is recommended. The app is 50–80 MB, but cached data and downloaded ePasses take extra space.
<h2>App setup and registration — first time</h2>
<h3>Step 1: Open the app</h3>
On first launch you get a language choice — Hindi/English/Telugu.
<h3>Step 2: Register / Login</h3>
<ul>
 	<li><strong>New user:</strong> tap "Register" → enter mobile number → verify OTP → fill name, email, state → done</li>
 	<li><strong>Existing TTD user:</strong> tap "Login" → the same mobile number you use on the website → verify OTP → straight to the dashboard</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The app and website use the <strong>same account</strong>. If you already registered on the TTD website, log in with the same mobile number — do not create a new registration, or your booking history won't show.
<h3>Step 3: Complete your profile</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Full name (as on the ID proof)</li>
 	<li>Date of birth</li>
 	<li>ID proof type and number (Aadhaar recommended)</li>
 	<li>Address (state and city)</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Fill the ID proof details correctly — the spelling must match your Aadhaar exactly. Then it auto-fills at booking time, so you won't re-enter it manually.
<h2>Booking a darshan ticket on the app</h2>
<h3>SED ₹300 booking (the most common)</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Home screen → "Darshan Booking" or "Special Entry Darshan"</li>
 	<li>A calendar shows available dates (green = available, red = full, grey = not open)</li>
 	<li>Select a date → available slots appear (morning/afternoon/evening)</li>
 	<li>Select a slot → add pilgrim details (up to 6 per booking)</li>
 	<li>Confirm ID proof details</li>
 	<li>Payment: UPI / card / net banking</li>
 	<li>On success, the ePass is generated automatically and appears under "My Bookings"</li>
</ul>
It's the same process as the website — the app interface is just cleaner, and <a href="/ttd-darshan-dates-kaise-check-karen/">checking available dates</a> is faster.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> On release day, when new slots open, the app gets very slow (so does the website). Crashes from server load are common — be patient and keep retrying.
<h3>Seva booking</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Home → "Seva Booking" → select the seva (Kalyanotsavam, Abhishekam, etc.)</li>
 	<li>Select an available date → pilgrim details</li>
 	<li>Payment → confirmation</li>
</ul>
The <a href="/ttd-seva-ticket-booking/">TTD Seva ticket booking</a> process is the same — the app just gives a better interface.
<h2>App features that beat the website</h2>
<h3>1. ePass auto-save</h3>
On the website you download the ePass manually. In the app, the <a href="/ttd-epass-kya-hai/">ePass auto-saves</a> to "My Bookings" the moment booking completes, and it stays accessible offline — so even with no network on Tirumala, the ePass shows.
<h3>2. Slot reminder notifications</h3>
A day before and 2 hours before, the app sends reminders:
<ul>
 	<li>"Your darshan slot is tomorrow at 9:00 AM"</li>
 	<li>"Reporting time: 7:00 AM at Gate X"</li>
 	<li>The reporting gate name is included too</li>
</ul>
<h3>3. Live queue status</h3>
Real-time queue info on the app:
<ul>
 	<li>Current waiting time (approximate)</li>
 	<li>Which compartments are active</li>
 	<li>Break timings</li>
</ul>
<h3>4. Accommodation booking</h3>
<a href="/ttd-accommodation-online-booking/">TTD guest house and cottage booking</a> is faster on the app — and you get a notification when cancellations free up rooms.
<h3>5. Prasadam booking</h3>
<a href="/ttd-laddu-online-order/">TTD laddu ordering</a> can be done directly in the app, with home delivery or Tirupati pickup options.
<h2>💡 Gap — the app's limitations (honest review)</h2>
Not everything is perfect. The app's real issues:
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Crashes at release time:</strong> when new dates open (usually month start), the app is as slow/crash-prone as the website</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>OTP delay:</strong> sometimes the OTP is 2–3 minutes late — login can be frustrating</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Payment gateway hiccups:</strong> UPI payments occasionally fail and need a retry</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Slow on older phones:</strong> on phones with under 3 GB RAM, the app lags</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Incomplete Hindi interface:</strong> some sections are still English/Telugu only</li>
</ul>
My honest take: the app is an 8/10 — solid for daily use, but at high-demand booking times, keep both the website and the app open and book on whichever opens first.
<h2>💡 Gap — App vs Website: when to use which</h2>
<ul>
 	<li><strong>App is better for:</strong> ePass access, slot reminders, accommodation checks, quick status checks, on-the-go use</li>
 	<li><strong>Website is better for:</strong> first-time booking (a bigger screen helps), heavy-traffic times (the website is sometimes more stable), comparing across multiple tabs</li>
 	<li><strong>Both equally good:</strong> the payment process, seva booking, cancellation</li>
</ul>
🌅 <strong>Pro Tip:</strong> For a serious booking attempt (new dates releasing), keep both a laptop and the phone ready — try the app and website simultaneously and book on whichever loads first. Since both share the same account, there's no conflict.
<h2>Keep the app updated — important</h2>
The TTD app updates regularly, especially before festival seasons. On an outdated app:
<ul>
 	<li>New features may be missing</li>
 	<li>Payment errors can occur</li>
 	<li>Login issues can appear</li>
</ul>
Keep <strong>auto-update ON</strong>, or check manually a week before travel:
<ul>
 	<li>Android: Play Store → Manage apps → TTD → Update</li>
 	<li>iOS: App Store → Profile → Available Updates → TTD</li>
</ul>
<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Downloading a fake/unofficial TTD app:</strong> searching "TTD darshan" shows fake apps too. Confirm the developer is "Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams" — the rest are fraudulent and can put your data and payment info at risk.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Making separate accounts on the app and website:</strong> they share one account. Two accounts split your booking history and cause confusion.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Keeping app notifications OFF:</strong> the slot reminders are genuinely useful. Before your trip, ensure TTD app notifications are allowed in phone settings.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking from an outdated app version:</strong> old versions bring payment failures, login errors and missing features. Update before the trip.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Clearing the app's data from phone settings:</strong> doing this to "free storage" deletes the saved ePass and login session — you'll have to re-login, and the offline ePass is gone.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking over public WiFi:</strong> airport/railway-station WiFi is unsafe for payment details. Use mobile data or a trusted network for booking and payment.</li>
</ul>
