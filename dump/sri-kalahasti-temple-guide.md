<h2>Why is Sri Kalahasti famous?</h2>
<h3>1. Vayu Lingam — one of the five Pancha Bhuta Sthalas</h3>
Hinduism recognises five elements: earth, water, fire, air and sky. Each element has a Shiva temple representing it. Sri Kalahasti represents Vayu (air). The lingam here is said to be self-manifested, and a lamp inside the sanctum flickers without any wind source — a phenomenon devotees attribute to the presence of Vayu.

<h3>2. Rahu-Ketu Sarpa Dosha Puja</h3>
This is the biggest draw — especially for North Indians. If your horoscope shows Sarpa Dosha (an unfavourable position of Rahu and Ketu), this temple offers a systematic remedial puja. It is one of the rare temples in India where this puja is performed officially and in batches.
<h3>3. The name — Sri (Spider), Kala (Cobra), Hasti (Elephant)</h3>
The temple's name comes from a story: a spider, a cobra and an elephant each worshipped Shiva here in their own way — the spider by weaving a protective web, the cobra by offering jewels, and the elephant by pouring water. Shiva granted all three moksha.

<h2>Rahu-Ketu Sarpa Dosha Puja — what it is and how to book</h2>
<h3>What is the puja?</h3>
For those whose kundali shows Sarpa Dosha (Rahu and Ketu in unfavourable positions), this puja is a remedial ritual performed by specially trained priests. Duration is typically 1.5–3 hours depending on the type.

Always book at the temple's <strong>official puja counter</strong> — avoid touts and outside agents offering "special packages" at inflated prices. The genuine puja happens inside the temple premises, not outside.
<h3>How to book</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>✅ <strong>Online (recommended):</strong> advance booking is available on the temple's official site (srikalahasti.org) — especially useful on weekends and auspicious days when batches fill up.</li>
 	<li>✅ <strong>On-spot:</strong> same-day registration at the temple counter — arrive early morning.</li>
 	<li>✅ <strong>Keep ready:</strong> your gothram, full name, and Rahu-Ketu position from your kundali. If you do not have a kundali, the priest can work with your birth date, time and place.</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Online booking gives you a confirmation letter — print it and carry it. At the counter you get priority. Without it, especially on a weekend, you might wait 1–2 hours for the next batch.
<h2>💡 Gap — avoiding puja touts</h2>
Outside the temple and at the bus stand, agents will approach offering "special puja" at various prices, claiming faster access or "VIP seating." These are not official. The correct process is:
<ul>
 	<li>Walk past them — politely decline.</li>
 	<li>Go to the temple's official puja counter inside the premises.</li>
 	<li>Book at the listed rates displayed on the board.</li>
</ul>
The official puja is systematic, transparent and performed by temple-appointed priests.

<h2>Temple architecture — what you will see</h2>
Sri Kalahasti temple is massive — a 120-foot Dravidian gopuram, multiple mandapams, and a location on the banks of the Swarnamukhi river. The temple dates from the 5th–11th century, developed by the Pallava and Vijayanagara dynasties.

Inside the complex:
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Main sanctum:</strong> Srikalahasteeswara (Shiva Lingam)</li>
 	<li><strong>Gnana Prasunamba shrine:</strong> Goddess Parvati</li>
 	<li><strong>Rahu-Ketu Puja mandapam:</strong> the special puja hall</li>
 	<li><strong>Swarnamukhi river ghat:</strong> for sacred bath before puja</li>
 	<li><strong>100-pillar mandapam:</strong> architectural highlight</li>
</ul>
<h2>💡 Gap — Kalamkari art souvenir</h2>
Srikalahasti is famous for Kalamkari — a traditional hand-painted fabric art that is UNESCO-recognised. Authentic pieces are available in shops around the temple and the main bazaar.
<ul>
 	<li>Price range: ₹200–₹2,000+ depending on size and detail</li>
 	<li>What to look for: genuine hand-painted (not printed) pieces with natural dyes</li>
 	<li>Keep 1–2 extra hours if you plan to shop</li>
</ul>
This is a genuinely unique souvenir — unlike mass-market temple merchandise.
