<h2>What is a seva, and how is it different from darshan?</h2>
Many assume booking a seva automatically gets you darshan. It doesn't. Darshan means viewing the deity inside the temple; a seva means taking part in a specific ritual as a sponsor — for example, in Kalyanotsavam you and your spouse symbolically play the role of the celebrants in the Lord's divine wedding.

After most sevas you do get a darshan from near the sanctum — but that's a bonus, not the main purpose, and a few sevas don't include it. So treat seva and darshan as two separate bookings. The full menu is in the <a href="/ttd-arjitha-sevas-list/">Arjitha sevas list</a>, and how darshan itself works is in <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">types of Tirumala darshan</a>.

<h2>💡 Gap: the Electronic DIP (Lucky Dip) — the big 2026 change</h2>
This is what most guides miss. For the most in-demand sevas — Suprabhatam, Thomala, Archana and Ashtadala Pada Padmaradhana — TTD no longer sells tickets first-come-first-served. They're allotted through an <strong>Electronic DIP (Lucky Dip)</strong>, essentially a lottery:
<ol>
 	<li><strong>Register</strong> for the e-dip during the announced window (registration is open for a couple of days).</li>
 	<li><strong>Selection</strong> happens by random draw — money doesn't help, luck does.</li>
 	<li>If selected, you <strong>pay within the given window</strong> to confirm the ticket.</li>
</ol>
So the old advice — "set an alarm, slots fill in 1–2 hours" — is outdated for these sevas. You enter the draw and wait for the result. Other sevas (Kalyanotsavam, Unjal Seva, Arjitha Brahmotsavam, Sahasra Deepalankara) are still released as direct online bookings on their announced date.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The monthly seva quota and e-dip dates are announced in advance (often a couple of months ahead). Always check the current schedule on <a href="https://www.tirumala.org/" rel="nofollow">tirumala.org</a> before planning around a specific seva.

<h2>How to book a seva — step by step</h2>
<ol>
 	<li><strong>Register/login</strong> on the official TTD portal (tirupatibalaji.ap.gov.in) or the <a href="/ttd-mobile-app-guide/">TTD app</a>. Use your name exactly as on Aadhaar.</li>
 	<li><strong>Open Arjitha Sevas</strong> and check the calendar for your seva and date.</li>
 	<li><strong>For Lucky Dip sevas:</strong> register in the e-dip window; if selected, pay within the payment window to confirm.</li>
 	<li><strong>For direct sevas:</strong> select the date/batch, fill pilgrim details, and pay.</li>
 	<li><strong>Pay</strong> by UPI/card and save the confirmation (screenshot + printout).</li>
 	<li><strong>On seva day,</strong> reach the seva counter early with original ID for every participant.</li>
</ol>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> For Kalyanotsavam, both spouses' details must match their Aadhaar exactly. For couples, see the dedicated <a href="/kalyanotsavam-seva-guide/">Kalyanotsavam guide</a>; for the pre-dawn experience, the <a href="/suprabhatam-seva-booking/">Suprabhatam guide</a> and <a href="/thomala-seva-guide/">Thomala Seva guide</a> help.

<h2>Dress code &amp; seva day</h2>
Dress enforcement is stricter for sevas than for regular darshan — men in dhoti with kurta, women in a saree or churidar with dupatta; no jeans, trousers or sleeveless. Reach the counter early, carry original ID for each participant, and keep your confirmation handy. Full rules are in the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">dress code guide</a>.
<h2>Offline booking, cancellation &amp; brokers</h2>
A limited offline option exists at TTD counters (popular sevas rarely available; simple ones like Archana sometimes are), but online is the reliable route. Seva tickets are generally non-refundable and rescheduling isn't usually allowed, so confirm your date before booking.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> TTD repeatedly warns against booking sevas through brokers or middlemen. Book only on the official portal — anyone "guaranteeing" a Lucky Dip seva for extra money is a scam. For current rates, timings and any complaint, use the official channels listed on tirumala.org.
<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — TTD seva booking</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Treating the seva ticket as a darshan ticket:</strong> They're separate. Book <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">darshan</a> separately.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>"Setting an alarm" for Lucky Dip sevas:</strong> Suprabhatam, Thomala, Archana and Ashtadala are by lottery now — register in the window instead.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Name mismatch with Aadhaar:</strong> Mismatched names can be refused at the seva counter.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Arriving late:</strong> Seva counters close ahead of time — late means no entry, no refund.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Ignoring the stricter seva dress code:</strong> Regular clothes won't do; men need a dhoti.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Assuming a fixed price from an old blog:</strong> Several seva prices have changed (e.g., Kalyanotsavam ~₹1,000) — verify on tirumala.org.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking through a broker:</strong> Use only the official portal; brokers selling sevas are a scam.</li>
</ul>
