<h2>First, understand this — Tirupati and Tirumala are NOT the same</h2>
This is probably the most common Tirupati confusion in India, and it must be cleared first.

<strong>Tirupati</strong> = a city. The railway station is here. The hotels are here. The temple is about 22 km away from here.

<strong>Tirumala</strong> = the hill where Lord Venkateswara's famous temple stands. It is 45–60 minutes from Tirupati by bus or taxi.

In short: booking a hotel in Tirupati does not mean you have "arrived" — you still travel up to Tirumala separately.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Most pilgrims sleep in Tirupati town and go up to Tirumala in the morning. But the ideal plan includes one night on Tirumala itself — so early-morning sevas and a peaceful darshan become possible.

<h2>Step 1 — Decide your travel dates</h2>
<h3>Best time: January–February and June–July</h3>
Lower crowds, pleasant weather, and the SED ticket is easy to get.
<h3>Avoid: May, September–October (Brahmotsavam), December (Vaikunta Ekadasi)</h3>
On these dates the crowd is extreme, tickets are scarce, and the experience is rushed.
<h3>Weekday vs weekend</h3>
Always choose a weekday (Monday–Thursday) — in the same month, the weekend queue is roughly double.

See the <a href="/tirupati-best-time-to-visit/">month-by-month crowd and weather guide</a>, and for festival dates, the <a href="/brahmotsavam-2026-dates-schedule/">Brahmotsavam 2026 dates guide</a>.
<h2>Step 2 — How to reach Tirupati</h2>
<h3>Train (best for North Indians)</h3>
Direct trains run from Delhi, Patna, Jaipur and many cities; 3AC is about ₹1,500–₹2,000 one way, journey 28–32 hours. The AP Sampark Kranti Express (12707, from New Delhi) and the Andhra Pradesh Express (12723) are the popular options. From Lucknow direct trains are limited and often involve a change — see the city guide.

See the <a href="/delhi-se-tirupati/">Delhi to Tirupati complete guide</a> and the <a href="/lucknow-to-tirupati/">Lucknow to Tirupati route guide</a>.
<h3>Flight</h3>
Tirupati has its own airport — about 2 hours from Delhi, ₹3,000–₹8,000 one way on advance booking.
<h3>Booking tip</h3>
Book train tickets about 2 months ahead — in peak season 3AC fills fast. Use the IRCTC app or website, and always confirm the destination station is Tirupati (TPTY), since some trains terminate at Renigunta (RU).
<h2>Step 3 — Book your TTD darshan ticket</h2>
This is the most critical booking — and where most people go wrong.
<h3>The 2 main darshan options</h3>
<strong>Sarva Darshan (free):</strong> no ticket; join the queue directly. Wait: 6–20+ hours. It now uses the SSD token system, with tokens collected at Tirupati counters. For budget travellers.

<strong>SED — Special Entry Darshan ₹300:</strong> advance online booking. Wait: 2–4 hours. Recommended for everyone.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The SED ticket is online-only — released as a monthly quota on a pre-announced date (typically a couple of months ahead), and it sells out within minutes. There is no reliable same-day walk-in. Book the moment the month's quota opens.

See the <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">SED ₹300 booking step-by-step</a>, the <a href="/types-of-tirumala-darshan/">full list of darshan types</a>, and the <a href="/tirumala-darshan-waiting-time-2026-2026/">honest waiting-time guide</a>.
<h3>How darshan booking works</h3>
tirumala.org → "Online Services" → "Darshan Booking" → select date and slot → pilgrim details → payment. To check open dates, see the <a href="/how-to-check-ttd-darshan-availability/">how to check available dates guide</a>.
<h2>Step 4 — Book Tirumala accommodation</h2>
For a 2+ day plan, one night on Tirumala is strongly recommended.

<strong>Why?</strong> Commuting up and down from town daily wastes 2–3 hours. Staying on the hill unlocks early-morning seva, a peaceful atmosphere and an unhurried experience.

<strong>Booking:</strong> tirumala.org → "Accommodation" → select date → payment. Book in advance, as rooms are released through the monthly online quota; rates run ₹500–₹3,000 by room type. TTD allots the room — you cannot pick a specific cottage.

See the <a href="/ttd-accommodation-online-booking/">TTD cottage booking complete guide</a>.
<h2>Step 5 — Book a Tirupati town hotel</h2>
Alongside the Tirumala cottage, book one night in Tirupati town for the arrival day.
<ul>
 	<li>Budget: ₹500–₹900 — Railway Station area lodges</li>
 	<li>TTD guest houses: ₹200–₹600 — advance online booking</li>
 	<li>Dharamshala: free–₹200 — state rest houses (UP, Bihar, Rajasthan)</li>
</ul>
See the <a href="/tirupati-budget-hotels-1000/">hotels under ₹1,000 list</a> and the <a href="/tirupati-dharamshala-guide/">free and low-cost dharamshala guide</a>.
<h2>Step 6 — Optional: book sevas</h2>
Sevas are optional — but if you want one, book in advance.
<ul>
 	<li><a href="/kalyanotsavam-seva-guide/">Kalyanotsavam</a> — for couples, around ₹1,000 per couple</li>
 	<li><a href="/tirumala-abhishekam-seva-guide/">Abhishekam</a> — from a few hundred to a few thousand rupees</li>
 	<li>Sahasra Deepalankara — an evening, spectacular seva</li>
</ul>
Note: popular sevas now go through the Electronic Dip lottery. See the <a href="/ttd-arjitha-sevas-list/">full Arjitha Sevas list</a> and the <a href="/free-vs-paid-sevas-tirumala/">free vs paid sevas comparison</a>.
<h2>Step 7 — Arriving in Tirupati: Day 1 plan</h2>
<h3>Arrive in the morning</h3>
An overnight train getting you to Tirupati by 7–8 AM is ideal. Hotel check-in from the station, freshen up, breakfast.
<h3>Day 1 afternoon: head up to Tirumala</h3>
From the Tirupati Central Bus Stand, take the APSRTC Tirumala bus — ₹65–₹70, every 10–15 minutes, 45–60 minutes journey. See the <a href="/tirupati-to-tirumala/">Tirupati to Tirumala guide</a> and the <a href="/tirupati-local-transport-guide/">local transport guide</a>.
<h3>Day 1 evening: explore Tirumala</h3>
Swami Pushkarini, an evening seva, and the TTD Annadanam dinner. See the <a href="/tirumala-annadanam-guide/">Annadanam free meals guide</a>.
<h2>Step 8 — Day 2: darshan and return</h2>
<h3>Early morning: SED darshan</h3>
Wake at 4 AM. Reach the SED reporting point by ~5:15 AM (for a 6 AM slot). Aadhaar plus the ticket printout/screenshot are mandatory.
<h3>After darshan: laddu counter</h3>
Go straight there from the darshan exit. Every pilgrim generally gets one free laddu, and SED ticket holders typically receive additional laddus. See the <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">laddu guide</a>.
<h3>Returning from Tirumala</h3>
Bus ₹65–₹70; collect your luggage in Tirupati.
<h3>Optional Day 2 afternoon</h3>
<a href="/tiruchanur-padmavathi-temple-guide/">Tiruchanur Padmavathi Temple</a> (5 km) and <a href="/kapila-theertham-tirupati/">Kapila Theertham</a> (2 km) fit comfortably in the same afternoon.
<h2>Dress code — do not miss this</h2>
There is a dress check at the Tirumala reporting point. Wrong dress means no entry.
<ul>
 	<li>✅ Men: dhoti / veshti — or a formal kurta-pyjama</li>
 	<li>✅ Women: saree or salwar suit</li>
 	<li>❌ Jeans, shorts, sleeveless, western clothing — not allowed</li>
</ul>
See the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules-2026-2026/">complete dress code guide</a>.
<h2>Packing — what to carry</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>Aadhaar card original (for everyone)</li>
 	<li>All booking confirmations — print and clear screenshot</li>
 	<li>Dhoti (men) — also hireable in Tirupati for ₹50–₹100</li>
 	<li>A thin sheet or small mat — for the VQC wait</li>
 	<li>Power bank — charging on Tirumala is limited</li>
 	<li>Cash ₹3,000–₹5,000 per person</li>
 	<li>Water bottle, light snacks</li>
</ul>

<h2>❌ Common mistakes first-timers make</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Treating Tirupati and Tirumala as the same:</strong> Tirupati = town, Tirumala = temple, ~22 km apart. A town hotel does not put you "near the temple."</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not booking the SED ticket in advance:</strong> "we'll get it there" is the most dangerous assumption — the online monthly-quota booking is the only reliable route.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Skipping a Tirumala night:</strong> daily up-and-down from town wastes 2–3 hours. Book a cottage in advance.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Preparing the dress code last-minute:</strong> wrong dress at the reporting point means no entry — it wrecks the whole plan.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not checking festival dates:</strong> a trip that lands mid-Brahmotsavam or Vaikunta Ekadasi without 3-month planning becomes a nightmare.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Buying "Tirupati Laddu" in town:</strong> the authentic TTD laddu is only at the Tirumala counter — the town version is an imitation.</li>
</ul>
