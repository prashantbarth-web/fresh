<h2>What actually changed in 2024–2026</h2>
If you only remember one thing from this article, remember this: the two rules people get wrong most are the ones that changed recently — the free darshan token and the ₹300 ticket release.

Earlier, free Sarva Darshan meant "just walk up and join the line." Now it runs on a Slotted Sarva Darshan (SSD) system — you collect a token, with a time slot, before you can enter the queue. And the popular ₹300 Special Entry Darshan is no longer a simple "book 45 days ahead at midnight." TTD releases it as a monthly online quota on a pre-announced date.

💡 <strong>Gap:</strong> Most older guides still describe a midnight 45-day SED window and a walk-in free queue. Both are outdated. The single most useful 2026 habit is to check the TTD <a href="/tirumala-darshan-token-system-2026-epass-vs-physical-token-vs-online-slot-2026-epass-vs-physical-token-vs-online-slot/">darshan token system</a> and the current month's quota dates before you plan anything.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Rule tightening usually happens around festivals — Brahmotsavam, Vaikunta Ekadasi, long weekends. If your trip falls near those dates, re-check everything a few days before you leave.

<h2>Mobile phone and electronics rules</h2>
The no-phone policy has only got stricter, and in 2026 it is fully enforced. Security checks are thorough, so do not try to slip a phone through.
<ul>
 	<li>❌ Mobile phones — must be deposited before you enter the queue complex.</li>
 	<li>❌ Cameras (DSLR or compact) — not allowed.</li>
 	<li>❌ Smart watches, wireless earbuds and similar devices — generally not allowed inside.</li>
 	<li>✅ Free lockers are available near the queue complex — no charge.</li>
 	<li>✅ Keep the paper locker receipt safe; you need it to collect your items.</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> If a phone is found hidden in your bag, you can be sent back out and lose your queue slot. See the <a href="/tirumala-mobile-locker-guide/">Tirumala mobile and locker guide</a> for how the deposit system works.

<h2>Ticket and token rules 2026 — Special Entry, Sarva, Divya</h2>
<h3>Special Entry Darshan (₹300)</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Online booking only — through the official TTD portal or app. There is no offline counter sale.</li>
 	<li>Released as a monthly quota on an announced date (generally a couple of months ahead), not a fixed 45-day window.</li>
 	<li>Slot timing is enforced — reach well before your slot; latecomers can be refused.</li>
 	<li>Non-transferable — the named person must show the matching ID.</li>
</ul>
For the full process, see the <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">TTD ₹300 Special Entry Darshan booking guide</a>.
<h3>Sarva Darshan (free) — now SSD</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Free, but it now runs on the Slotted Sarva Darshan (SSD) token system.</li>
 	<li>Tokens are collected at Tirupati counters (such as Srinivasam, Vishnu Nivasam and Bhudevi Complex) with Aadhaar/biometric verification, on a first-come-first-served basis.</li>
 	<li>Tokens are often for the next day; the token carries a reporting slot.</li>
 	<li>Waiting time with a token is generally several hours; without a token (the non-token line) it can stretch much longer on busy days.</li>
</ul>
Details are in the <a href="/ttd-sarva-darshan-ticket-booking/">Sarva Darshan booking guide</a>.
<h3>Divya Darshan (footpath pilgrims)</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>For pilgrims walking up via Alipiri or Srivari Mettu — a token is issued on the footpath.</li>
 	<li>It generally gives a shorter wait than the regular free line.</li>
 	<li>Footwear is deposited at the temple cloak room.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Special categories — seniors, differently-abled, children</h2>
TTD provides separate arrangements for some groups, and these are genuinely worth using rather than standing in the general line.
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Senior citizens:</strong> a dedicated darshan provision with limited daily quota; carry age proof, and one companion is generally allowed. The full details are in the <a href="/senior-citizens-special-facities/">senior citizens Tirumala darshan guide</a>.</li>
 	<li><strong>Differently-abled pilgrims:</strong> wheelchair help and special entry support are available — it helps to inform staff in advance.</li>
 	<li><strong>Children:</strong> very young children enter with parents; for older children, carry an ID such as a school ID or birth certificate where possible.</li>
</ul>
<h2>Prasadam and Laddu rules</h2>
The famous Tirumala <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">Laddu Prasadam</a> follows its own simple rules at the counters.
<ul>
 	<li>Special Entry Darshan ticket holders generally receive free laddus after darshan.</li>
 	<li>Extra laddus are sold at a separate counter; price and the per-person carry limit are set by TTD and change from time to time.</li>
 	<li>Outside prasadam is not allowed inside.</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Laddu price and carry limits are revised periodically — check current rates on <a href="https://tirumala.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">tirumala.org</a> rather than relying on an old figure.
