<h2>TTD accommodation — where it is and options</h2>
TTD runs accommodation in two places — Tirumala (up the hill, near the temple) and Tirupati (in the city below). Tirumala rooms are walking distance from the temple and ideal for early-morning darshan, but they're limited and the first to fill. Tirupati rooms are more available and cheaper, but you then travel up to the temple by bus or the ghat road.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If you've planned an early-morning darshan or seva (3–4 AM), book Tirumala accommodation — getting up from Tirupati at that hour is very hard. There are no private hotels on the hill, so for staying up top, TTD is the only option; the details are in the <a href="/ttd-tirumala-cottages-booking/">Tirumala cottages booking guide</a>.

<h2>💡 Gap: you now need a darshan/seva ticket to book a room</h2>
This is the 2026 change most guides (and older versions of this article) get wrong. The old line — "a darshan ticket isn't mandatory to book accommodation" — is no longer accurate. Booking a Tirumala room online is now generally <strong>linked to holding a darshan or seva ticket</strong>.

So the order matters:
<ol>
 	<li>Sort your <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">darshan</a> (or seva) booking first.</li>
 	<li>Then book the room against it on the portal.</li>
</ol>
For a ₹300 Special Entry Darshan, accommodation is typically opened for two days around your darshan date (the previous day and the day itself). Because these rules keep changing, confirm the current requirement on tirumala.org before planning.
<h2>💡 Gap: you don't pick a complex — TTD allots a room (and the CRO option)</h2>
Another thing pilgrims expect but don't get: you can't choose a specific complex like "Srinivasam" or "Ram Bagicha" on the portal. You select a room type and dates, pay, and TTD <strong>allots an available room</strong> — you collect the actual allotment at the Internet Allotment/ARP counter near the CRO (Central Reception Office) in Tirumala.

If online rooms are sold out, there's a legitimate offline route: the <strong>CRO counter</strong> near the Tirumala bus stand does on-the-spot "current booking" from early morning based on real-time vacancy. Stock runs out within an hour or two, so reach early. It's a genuine fallback when the portal shows nothing.
<h2>How to book online — step by step</h2>
<ol>
 	<li><strong>Log in</strong> to the official TTD portal (ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in) — register with mobile/OTP if new; use your name exactly as on Aadhaar.</li>
 	<li><strong>Open Accommodation</strong> and choose location — Tirumala or Tirupati.</li>
 	<li><strong>Select dates and room type</strong> (available shows bookable); add the number of occupants.</li>
 	<li><strong>Enter pilgrim details</strong> for the primary guest and family.</li>
 	<li><strong>Pay</strong> by UPI/card and download the confirmation — keep a printout.</li>
 	<li><strong>Check in</strong> at the allotment counter near the CRO with original ID for all adults; check-in is generally from noon.</li>
</ol>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Tirumala cottages usually allow a maximum of 4–6 persons; for more, book two rooms. Carry original Aadhaar for every adult — a photocopy alone isn't accepted.
<h2>Availability — when you'll get a room (and when not)</h2>
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Peak season</strong> (Oct–Jan, Brahmotsavam, long weekends): Tirumala rooms can sell out the moment the ~90-day quota opens. Book the instant the window opens.</li>
 	<li><strong>Regular season</strong> (Feb–Jun weekdays): rooms often available 2–3 weeks out; Tirupati options almost always.</li>
 	<li><strong>Monsoon</strong> (Jul–Sep): lighter crowds, better availability.</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Cancellations free up rooms — keep checking the portal for your dates, as last-minute slots do open. Watch the <a href="/ttd-accommodation-reform-news/">accommodation reform updates</a> for any rule changes.
<h2>If you don't get a TTD room</h2>
Don't panic — you have options. Book a <a href="/tirupati-budget-hotels-1000/">budget hotel in Tirupati</a> (₹800–₹2,500) or a <a href="/tirupati-dharamshala-guide/">dharamshala</a>, and travel up by bus — see <a href="/tirupati-se-tirumala-kaise-jayen/">getting to Tirumala</a>. Or shift your dates to a weekday for far better availability. Or take a <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">darshan slot</a> and do a same-day return without staying over. For more hill options, see the <a href="/tirumala-stay-options-pilgrims/">Tirumala stay options guide</a>.
<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — TTD accommodation booking</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking the room before the darshan ticket:</strong> In 2026 the room is generally tied to a darshan/seva ticket — sort that first.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Confusing Tirumala and Tirupati rooms:</strong> They're separate locations — pick the right one when booking.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Expecting to choose a specific complex:</strong> TTD allots an available room; you collect it at the CRO/allotment counter.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Ignoring maximum occupancy:</strong> Bringing 6 to a 4-person room causes problems at check-in.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking on a third-party site:</strong> Only the official TTD portal works — agents/lookalike sites are a fraud risk.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Carrying only a photocopy:</strong> Original ID for every adult is required at check-in.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Leaving it for peak-season last minute:</strong> Book the moment the ~90-day window opens.</li>
</ul>
