<h2>💡 Gap — packaging: what arrives, and how to verify it</h2>
The online proforma laddu is specially packaged — different from the counter laddu, made courier-safe.

<strong>What to expect:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>A crush-resistant cardboard outer box</li>
 	<li>Individual inner wrapping — moisture protection</li>
 	<li>An official TTD label printed with the order details</li>
 	<li>An authenticity seal / hologram sticker</li>
 	<li>Manufacture and best-before dates clearly printed</li>
</ul>
✅ <strong>How to confirm it is authentic:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>Official TTD seal and logo</li>
 	<li>A hologram sticker that shimmers in light</li>
 	<li>A printed label — not handwritten</li>
 	<li>A clearly mentioned best-before date</li>
</ul>
❌ <strong>Red flags — complain if:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>The box is crushed or the seal is broken</li>
 	<li>The laddu has an unusual smell — rancid or sour</li>
 	<li>There is no official TTD label</li>
 	<li>The best-before date has already passed</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Inspect the packaging in front of the courier at the time of delivery. If damage is visible, have a "delivery with damage" note recorded — it helps with any complaint.

<strong>Season-wise storage:</strong>

🌡️ <strong>Summer (April–June):</strong> at 40°C+ in North India the laddu turns soft and sticky in 3–4 days. Refrigerate the moment it arrives.

🌧️ <strong>Monsoon (July–September):</strong> humidity is absorbed quickly — an airtight container is essential.

❄️ <strong>Winter (November–February):</strong> easily 10 days at room temperature — the best season to receive a laddu delivery.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Delivery happens in office hours, usually 10 AM–6 PM. If no one is home, instruct a neighbour or building security to receive it.

<h2>Specific tips for North Indians</h2>
✅ <strong>Plan from the delivery time, not the occasion:</strong> for a housewarming, birthday or anniversary, order 3–4 weeks ahead. For UP/Bihar, a 4-week margin is safe.

✅ <strong>Keep the refrigerator ready in summer:</strong> April–June, refrigerate on arrival — not room temperature.

✅ <strong>Always add a landmark:</strong> "Near XYZ School" or "Opposite ABC Temple" — North Indian couriers rely on landmarks.

✅ <strong>Stay reachable in the daytime:</strong> delivery is 10 AM–6 PM — if you are at work, keep someone home or alert a neighbour.

✅ <strong>For a larger quantity, order within the portal limit:</strong> if you need more than the limit allows, plan ahead and place a fresh order later rather than trying to work around the limit, which TTD sets for fair access.
<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Ordering a week before a festival:</strong> North India needs 10–12 days minimum in normal season, 15–20 in festival season. One week is not enough.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Trying to track via a random courier app:</strong> TTD uses its own courier partner — there is no Swiggy/Dunzo delivery. The official tracking link is in your email.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Leaving it at room temperature in summer:</strong> beyond 3 days in May–June heat, the laddu turns soft and sticky.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Confusing working days with calendar days:</strong> "5 working days" excludes Sundays and holidays — actual calendar days can be more.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not reporting damage immediately:</strong> after 24 hours, resolution gets harder. Inspect at the time of delivery.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Entering a wrong PIN code:</strong> the PIN drives routing — one wrong digit can send the parcel to the wrong city. Double-check it.</li>
</ul>
