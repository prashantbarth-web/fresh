Content:You step out of Tirupati Railway Station and a wall of auto drivers closes in. "Tirumala? ₹500!" "Bus Stand? ₹200!" "Sir, AC cab, best price!" For a first-time pilgrim it is overwhelming — you have no idea what the right fare is, what's a scam, or where the bus even leaves from.

This guide gives you exactly the information nobody hands you at the station: the real fares versus what drivers quote, how to negotiate without an argument, where each bus goes from, and the handful of common scams to sidestep.

None of this is complicated once you know the numbers. Tirupati's transport is actually well-organised and pilgrim-friendly — the problems come only from arriving uninformed and accepting the first inflated quote.

I have moved around Tirupati by auto, city bus and app cab across many trips. The single best habit: know the fair fare before you negotiate, and be ready to walk to the next auto. So treat the fares below as a practical baseline, then confirm locally, since rates shift with fuel and season.
<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — Tirupati local transport:
<ul>
 	<li>🛺 Auto: ₹40–₹150 within town — negotiate, meters are rare.</li>
 	<li>🚌 APSRTC city bus: ₹10–₹25 — the cheapest, best once you know the routes.</li>
 	<li>📱 Ola/Uber: available with consistent pricing, but patchy availability early/late.</li>
 	<li>🚐 Tirumala bus: from the Central Bus Stand (₹65–₹70) — NOT from the station.</li>
 	<li>✈️ Airport taxi: ₹250–₹500 — negotiate or use Ola.</li>
 	<li>⚠️ Common scam: drivers ask ₹300–₹500 for the Bus Stand, which should be ₹50–₹70.</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>

<h3>💡 Gap — the right way to negotiate</h3>
❌ <strong>Wrong:</strong> "Will you go to Tiruchanur?" — the driver names a high rate, you counter, and a long argument follows.

✅ <strong>Right:</strong> "Tiruchanur — ₹120?" — name your specific amount first. If he refuses, move to the next auto. By the third or fourth, you get the fair rate.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Walk a little away from the station entrance — autos on the side road are noticeably more reasonable. The ones right at the entrance charge a tourist premium.
<h3>Shared autos</h3>
On some fixed routes, shared autos run at ₹15–₹30 per person:
<ul>
 	<li>Railway Station → Alipiri: ₹20–₹25 per person</li>
 	<li>Bus Stand area → Tiruchanur: ₹20–₹30 per person</li>
</ul>
They take a little longer (multiple drop-offs) but are great on a budget.

<h2>Transport up on Tirumala</h2>
Private vehicles are restricted on the Tirumala hill. Options up there:
<ul>
 	<li>✅ TTD free bus — between points within the temple complex.</li>
 	<li>✅ Walking — the complex is mostly walkable; wear comfortable shoes.</li>
 	<li>✅ TTD special bus — for specific accommodation locations.</li>
 	<li>❌ Autos do not run on Tirumala.</li>
 	<li>❌ Private cars/bikes are restricted in upper Tirumala.</li>
</ul>
<h2>💡 Gap — common transport scams to avoid</h2>
<strong>Scam 1 — inflated auto fare at the station:</strong> "Bus Stand? ₹200!" is about 4x. The fair rate is ₹50–₹70. Negotiate firmly, or walk in a couple of minutes and take another auto.

<strong>Scam 2 — "Tirumala taxi" at the station:</strong> "Direct Tirumala taxi ₹800!" The APSRTC bus from the Bus Stand is ₹65–₹70. Unless you're a large family or convenience is the priority, you don't need the taxi.

<strong>Scam 3 — tourist package cab:</strong> "Tirumala + Tiruchanur + Kalahasti package ₹5,000!" is overpriced. A fair cab from your hotel is ₹2,500–₹3,000.

<strong>Scam 4 — the "buy laddu on the way" auto:</strong> some drivers take you to a town shop selling "authentic Tirupati laddu" on commission. The authentic TTD laddu comes only from the counter on Tirumala.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Most Tirupati auto drivers are honest — scams are the minority. Staying informed makes them easy to avoid.
<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Taking the first auto at the station:</strong> the entrance autos are the most aggressive and charge a premium. Walk 50 metres in, compare 3–4 options, then book.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Looking for the Tirumala bus at the station:</strong> it leaves from the Central Bus Stand, not the station — almost every other pilgrim gets this wrong.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Relying on Ola/Uber early morning:</strong> at 4–5 AM they're scarce. For an early Tirumala bus, stay near the Bus Stand the night before, or go by auto.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Asking for the meter:</strong> Tirupati autos don't really use meters — negotiating is the local system.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not planning the return trip:</strong> from Tiruchanur or Kapila Theertham, decide the return auto in advance, or have the driver wait (₹20–₹30 extra).</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking a cab last-minute in festival season:</strong> during Brahmotsavam and Vaikunta Ekadasi, cabs surge. Book ahead via your hotel or a scheduled app ride.</li>
</ul>
