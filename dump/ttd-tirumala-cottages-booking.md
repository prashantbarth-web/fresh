<h2>What are TTD Tirumala cottages?</h2>
Up on Tirumala hill, where the Venkateswara Swami temple is, TTD runs all the accommodation itself. No private hotels, no OYO — everything is TTD-managed. These stays are loosely called "cottages," but there are actually several types, from basic single rooms and dormitories to larger suite-style units. All of them are booked through the official portal.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Don't picture a hill-station "cottage." This is pilgrim-grade accommodation — clean, functional and comfortable in the Tirumala context, but not luxury. Set expectations accordingly and you won't be disappointed.

<h2>💡 Gap: how the quota actually opens now (the part old guides get wrong)</h2>
Most older articles tell you rooms unlock "every night at midnight on a rolling 30-day window." In 2026 that's no longer how it works, and following it will leave you refreshing at the wrong time.

What TTD actually does now: it releases the accommodation quota for a whole month in one go, on a pre-announced date and time — often around 3 PM on the release day — typically a couple of months in advance, alongside the darshan quota for that month. For example, a September quota release was announced for a specific June date at 3 PM.

So the real strategy is:
<ul>
 	<li>Watch tirumala.org / TTD news for the monthly quota-release date and time for your travel month.</li>
 	<li>Be logged in and ready at that exact time — popular dates can sell out within minutes.</li>
 	<li>Keep your pilgrim details and a fast payment method (UPI) ready before the clock hits.</li>
</ul>
Because TTD keeps adjusting these schedules, always reconfirm the current release date on the official site rather than trusting a fixed time you read somewhere.
<h2>💡 Gap: you generally need a darshan ticket first</h2>
Here's the rule that trips up many pilgrims in 2026: booking a Tirumala room online is generally linked to holding a darshan or seva ticket. The old "book the room, arrange darshan separately later" approach has largely changed.

So sort your <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">darshan booking</a> first, then book the room against it. And remember — a confirmed room is not a confirmed darshan; they remain two parts of the same trip, and staying on the hill does not by itself shorten the <a href="/tirumala-darshan-waiting-time/">darshan waiting time</a>.
<h2>Step-by-step booking process</h2>
<ol>
 	<li><strong>Create an account:</strong> On the official TTD online services portal, register with your mobile number and Aadhaar; verify the OTP.</li>
 	<li><strong>Select dates and room type:</strong> Available rooms show as bookable; pick your type and hold it. You typically get a short payment window (10–15 minutes) before the hold is released.</li>
 	<li><strong>Enter pilgrim details:</strong> Fill every pilgrim's name and ID accurately — Aadhaar is verified at check-in.</li>
 	<li><strong>Pay:</strong> UPI, net banking or card. On success, you get a PDF confirmation by email/SMS — save a screenshot.</li>
 	<li><strong>At check-in:</strong> Carry the confirmation and original Aadhaar for all pilgrims.</li>
</ol>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> If the payment gateway hangs during a rush and money is deducted without a confirmation, don't panic or pay twice. Refunds or confirmations usually settle within 24–48 hours; you can also call the TTD helpline.
<h2>💡 Gap: avoid brokers — and your offline backup</h2>
TTD repeatedly cautions pilgrims not to book darshan or accommodation through brokers and middlemen, and runs a toll-free complaint line (155257). Anyone promising a "guaranteed" Tirumala room for extra cash is a risk — fake confirmations and inflated charges are common. Book only on the official portal.

If online rooms are sold out, there's a legitimate fallback: the <strong>CRO (Central Reception Office)</strong> near the Tirumala bus stand does on-the-spot "current booking" from early morning, allotting rooms by real-time vacancy. Stock runs out within an hour or two, so reach early — but it's a genuine, official option when the portal shows nothing.

<h2>Cancellation and refund</h2>
TTD's accommodation cancellation policy generally works on a sliding scale — a fuller refund the earlier you cancel, reducing as you approach check-in, with little or no refund very close to the date. Refunds process to the original payment method over several working days. Exact slabs can change, so check the current policy at booking; the broader refund process is covered in the <a href="/ttd-darshan-ticket-cancel-refund/">TTD cancellation &amp; refund guide</a>.
<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — booking a Tirumala cottage</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Following the old midnight-rolling advice:</strong> The quota now opens on an announced monthly date/time. Watch tirumala.org for it instead.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Skipping the darshan ticket:</strong> Tirumala rooms generally can't be booked without a darshan/seva ticket in 2026. Book darshan first.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Assuming a room means confirmed darshan:</strong> They're separate bookings. A cottage doesn't get you a darshan slot.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking through a broker:</strong> TTD warns against this (toll-free 155257). Use only the official portal.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Closing the browser mid-payment:</strong> If payment is pending and you close the tab, the held room is released. Finish in the same tab.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Ignoring check-out timing:</strong> TTD check-out is early (commonly by morning) and strict. Plan your day around it.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Carrying the wrong ID:</strong> Aadhaar verification is mandatory at check-in for all pilgrims — carry originals.</li>
</ul>
