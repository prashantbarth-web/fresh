<h2>💡 Gap — late-night arrival protocol</h2>
This is the situation most North Indian pilgrims actually face — a train arriving after 11 PM.
<ol>
 	<li><strong>Pre-book if you can:</strong> even a same-day OYO booking beats walking into the dark.</li>
 	<li><strong>No booking? Try the retiring room first:</strong> check the IRCTC app for a Tirupati retiring room — safe and on the premises.</li>
 	<li><strong>Then the closest hotels:</strong> 2–3 hotels sit within 50 m of the exit. Negotiate firmly — late-night "desperation premium" is real, so state a fixed price.</li>
 	<li><strong>Confirm a 24-hour front desk:</strong> some budget lodges shut reception after 11 PM. Confirm the check-in time when booking.</li>
 	<li><strong>Keep offline maps:</strong> download Tirupati offline on Google Maps — late-night network can be patchy.</li>
</ol>
<h2>Hotel booking tips — station area</h2>
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Book in advance:</strong> MakeMyTrip, Goibibo, OYO — walk-in rates run 30–50% higher.</li>
 	<li><strong>Peak-season awareness:</strong> Oct–Jan (pilgrimage season), Brahmotsavam and weekends double the rates. Book 15–30 days ahead.</li>
 	<li><strong>Check-in time:</strong> most hotels check in at 12 PM. Arriving 5–6 AM? Request "early check-in" in advance — it may cost ₹200–300 extra.</li>
 	<li><strong>Late-night arrival:</strong> confirm a 24-hour front desk for your booking time.</li>
 	<li><strong>Read recent reviews:</strong> on Google Maps, read the last 3 months' reviews rather than the star rating — hotels deteriorate or improve quickly.</li>
 	<li><strong>ID proof:</strong> original Aadhaar/Voter ID is mandatory at check-in. Carry two copies.</li>
</ul>
<h2>💡 Gap — avoiding station touts</h2>
At the station exit, men asking "hotel chahiye?" are commission agents — they'll put you in a ₹500 room and charge ₹1,000. The clean approach:
<ul>
 	<li>Ignore the touts and walk to your pre-booked hotel using the address in Google Maps.</li>
 	<li>Exit via the East (main) exit — most hotels are on that side.</li>
 	<li>Auto to a station-area hotel should be ₹30–50 max — refuse anyone quoting ₹100+.</li>
 	<li>Late at night, keep valuables on your body, not in a side bag.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Eating options in the station area</h2>
<ul>
 	<li><strong>Station canteen (IRCTC):</strong> on Platform 1 — basic South Indian meals, 24/7, ₹50–100</li>
 	<li><strong>Hotel restaurants:</strong> Annapurna, Vishnu Priya — full meals ₹100–200</li>
 	<li><strong>Street food:</strong> at the exit — idli, dosa, vada (₹20–50), freshest early morning</li>
 	<li><strong>Late night:</strong> limited after 11 PM — hotel room service or the station canteen are the reliable options</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Tirupati is a predominantly vegetarian city, and non-veg around the station is extremely limited. If you do eat non-veg, avoid it the day before going to Tirumala — the temple area is purely vegetarian, and a prepared body and mind make for a better darshan.
<h2>Station to Tirumala bus stand</h2>
The APSRTC bus stand (for Tirumala buses) is about 500 m (5–7 min walk) from the railway station. Station-area hotels are within walking distance of it — the single biggest advantage of staying here. From the bus stand, Tirumala buses run every 5–10 minutes (around ₹65–₹100). See the <a href="/tirupati-se-tirumala-kaise-jayen/">Tirupati to Tirumala guide</a>.
<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Walking in without a booking in peak season:</strong> Oct–Jan and festivals run the station area at 100% occupancy. No booking means either a grubby room or a 3x rate. Always book ahead.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Trusting station touts:</strong> "AC room ₹500 only" rarely matches reality. Ignore the agents and go to your own booking.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking the cheapest room on price alone:</strong> ₹300–400 rooms can have serious cleanliness, safety and bedbug issues. Keep a ₹600–800 minimum for a decent, safe stay.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Ignoring check-in time:</strong> arriving 5 AM with a 12 PM check-in means a 7-hour wait. Request early check-in in advance, or use a retiring room for the initial hours.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Leaving everything in the room and going to Tirumala without confirming checkout:</strong> miss the checkout time and you're charged an extra day. Ask reception for a "late checkout" — usually allowed for ₹200–300.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Booking the highest-rated hotel blindly:</strong> ratings can be manipulated. Read written reviews, especially recent negative ones.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not keeping couple check-in documents ready:</strong> some hotels in this conservative temple town ask unmarried couples for extra ID. Carry matching-surname ID or a marriage certificate, or use OYO (more relaxed policy).</li>
</ul>
