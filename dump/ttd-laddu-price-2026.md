This guide lays out every price clearly for 2026 — counter cost, online cost, the limit, and exactly who gets a free laddu — along with the small but important updates that older guides still get wrong.

<h2>Counter price — the ₹50 and ₹200 detail</h2>
<h3>Srivari Laddu — about ₹50</h3>
This is the most common laddu — the one almost every pilgrim takes.
<ul>
 	<li>Weight: ~175 grams</li>
 	<li>Price: ~₹50 per piece</li>
 	<li>Where: the TTD counter (or UPI kiosk) after the darshan exit on Tirumala</li>
 	<li>Limit: up to 2 extra per person, beyond the free one</li>
 	<li>Shelf life: 7–10 days at room temperature</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> A 175 g laddu for about ₹50 is genuinely subsidised — a sweet of this quality would cost far more in the market. TTD keeps the price deliberately low for pilgrims.
<h3>Astadala Padmavathi Laddu — about ₹200</h3>
The large laddu — ideal for gifting and family sharing.
<ul>
 	<li>Weight: ~750 grams</li>
 	<li>Price: ~₹200 per piece</li>
 	<li>Where: the Tirumala counter — sometimes a separate queue</li>
 	<li>Shelf life: 7–10 days</li>
 	<li>Stock: limited — sells out fast in peak season</li>
</ul>
⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The large laddu has limited stock, especially in peak season and on festivals. Head to the counter straight after darshan — wait too long and you may find it sold out.
<h2>Online price — the ₹200 breakdown</h2>
Through Proforma Laddu Booking on tirumala.org, with home delivery:
<ul>
 	<li>Price: ~₹200 per laddu — delivery included</li>
 	<li>Quality: identical to the counter laddu — same kitchen, same recipe</li>
 	<li>No need to visit Tirumala</li>
 	<li>Delivery: anywhere in India, 3–7 working days</li>
</ul>

<h2>💡 Gap — the free laddu rule (updated)</h2>
This is where older guides go wrong. Many still say "only ₹300 SED ticket holders get a free laddu." That is no longer the full picture.

Since 2020, TTD has generally given <strong>every pilgrim one small laddu free</strong> after darshan, handed out at the prasadam point near the temple exit — regardless of darshan type. Beyond that one free laddu, you can buy more (up to a per-person limit). Special Entry Darshan ₹300 ticket holders typically receive additional laddus as part of their experience.

<h2>Limit — how many laddus can you take?</h2>
<h3>From the counter</h3>
Generally one free laddu per pilgrim after darshan, plus <strong>up to 2 extra per person</strong>, bought at the counter or UPI kiosk. So a family of four can come away with several laddus in total.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The "extra" limit applies per person — and your ticket or Aadhaar is checked at the counter/kiosk, so trying to exceed it only causes friction.
<h3>From online</h3>
The maximum quantity per order is shown on the TTD portal at checkout, and it changes with seasonal demand — lower around festivals so more pilgrims get equal access.
<h3>If you need more</h3>
<ul>
 	<li>Each family member's own free + extra laddus add up across the group</li>
 	<li>Order additional laddus to your home via the online proforma route</li>
 	<li>A second Tirumala visit, if you are on a 2-day plan</li>
</ul>
<h2>💡 Gap — why TTD keeps the price so low</h2>
TTD is a non-profit religious trust. The actual production cost of a laddu is more than ₹50 — ghee, besan, cashews, raisins and cardamom are all premium ingredients. TTD covers the gap through temple donations and Hundi revenue.

In other words, when you buy a laddu for about ₹50, TTD is subsidising it. That is exactly why the price stays so low for pilgrims.
<h2>When can the price change?</h2>
TTD has made occasional revisions over the years. Price changes typically happen:
<ul>
 	<li>When raw-material costs (ghee, besan) rise</li>
 	<li>At the TTD Board's annual review</li>
 	<li>With major policy changes</li>
</ul>
✅ For current 2026 prices: tirumala.org → "Prasadam" section stays updated.
<h2>💡 Gap — price as a fake-laddu test</h2>
In Tirupati town and on various online platforms, "Tirupati Laddu" sells for ₹100–₹300 per piece. This is not the authentic TTD prasadam.

<strong>A simple test using price:</strong>
<ul>
 	<li>TTD counter = ~₹50 (small) / ~₹200 (large) — subsidised</li>
 	<li>Online TTD = ~₹200 (delivery included)</li>
 	<li>No outside seller can offer the genuine, GI-protected laddu</li>
</ul>
If someone sells an "authentic TTD laddu" anywhere other than the Tirumala counter or tirumala.org, it is not the real prasadam. The full picture is in the <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">complete Tirumala laddu prasad guide</a>.
