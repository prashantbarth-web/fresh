<h2>💡 Gap: the facility is now the fast Supadham entry (not a 2–4 hour counter)</h2>
Most older guides describe a "Foreign National Counter" with a 2–4 hour wait. In 2026 TTD operates a dedicated fast entry for overseas pilgrims — the <strong>Supadham (Supatham) Entry</strong> — which is much quicker, often around 20–45 minutes and generally within about 1.5 hours, and it's free for those who qualify.

You report at the special darshan complex (Supadham entrance), fill a short form with your personal and visa/passport details, submit the documents, and join the priority line. It's designed precisely for tired travellers arriving from abroad, so it's far gentler than the regular queue. The wider darshan picture is in <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">types of Tirumala darshan</a>, and queue realities in the <a href="/tirumala-darshan-waiting-time/">waiting time guide</a>.

<h2>💡 Gap: do NRIs with an Indian passport qualify?</h2>
This is the most confusing point, and the old hard line — "Indian passport means absolutely no NRI access" — is now outdated. In 2026, the Supadham entry is widely described as serving NRIs as well, not only foreign-passport holders.

<h2>Documents — the full checklist</h2>
Carry originals — photocopies or digital copies alone are generally not accepted at the counter.
<ul>
 	<li>✅ Original passport for <strong>every member</strong> — including children (a commonly forgotten one).</li>
 	<li>✅ OCI / PIO card, if applicable.</li>
 	<li>✅ Valid India visa or arrival stamp (for foreign passport holders).</li>
 	<li>✅ For NRIs: Indian passport plus proof of overseas residence/visa, as required.</li>
 	<li>✅ A photocopy set as backup.</li>
</ul>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Save PDFs of all documents on your phone and share them within the family group as a backup — but the originals are what the counter needs. The full travel checklist is in the <a href="/tirupati-packing-list/">Tirupati packing list</a>.
<h2>Step by step at Tirumala</h2>
<ol>
 	<li><strong>Reach Tirumala</strong> — from Tirupati, take the TTD/APSRTC bus or a cab up the ghat road (45–60 min). Coming from the airport? See the <a href="/tirupati-airport-guide/">Tirupati airport guide</a>.</li>
 	<li><strong>Go to the Supadham / special darshan complex</strong> and fill the form with your passport and visa details.</li>
 	<li><strong>Submit your documents</strong> — originals for every member — for verification.</li>
 	<li><strong>Join the priority line</strong> and have darshan, typically within ~20 minutes to 1.5 hours.</li>
</ol>
🌅 <strong>Timing tip:</strong> Reach early in the morning for the smoothest experience. Staff at the counter help in English and Hindi, so don't hesitate to ask.

<h2>Dress code — don't get stopped at the gate</h2>
The dress code applies to everyone, and overseas pilgrims often arrive in western wear and get turned away. Men: dhoti/veshti or kurta-pyjama (a shirt with formal trousers is generally fine for general darshan). Women: saree or salwar-kameez with a dupatta — no jeans, shorts, sleeveless or western dresses. Traditional wear can be bought/rented near the temple, but carrying your own is safer. Full details are in the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">dress code rules</a>.
<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — NRI / foreign darshan</h2>
<ul>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Carrying a photocopy instead of the original passport:</strong> The counter needs originals for every member.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Forgetting a child's passport:</strong> Every member, including young children, needs their original passport.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Assuming you can't use the fast entry as an NRI:</strong> NRIs may now qualify for Supadham — check current rules rather than heading straight to the long queue.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Expecting to book Supadham online:</strong> It's an on-arrival counter; book the ₹300 darshan online if you want certainty.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Ignoring the dress code:</strong> Western wear means no entry — carry traditional attire.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Not carrying cash:</strong> ATM queues can be long on the hill — withdraw some in Tirupati for prasadam and small costs.</li>
 	<li>❌ <strong>Relying on an old blog's rules:</strong> NRI/foreign darshan rules change — confirm on tirumala.org before you travel.</li>
</ul>
