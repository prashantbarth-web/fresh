Just across the Andhra-Tamil Nadu border, about an hour and a half from Tirupati, stands one of the holiest Murugan temples in all of South India — Tiruttani. It is one of the Arupadai Veedu, the six sacred abodes of Lord Murugan (Subramanya/Kartikeya), and for devotees of Murugan, being this close to it on a Tirupati trip and not visiting feels like a missed blessing.

Perched on a hill reached by a flight of about 365 steps (one for each day of the year, by tradition), the Tiruttani Subramanya Swamy temple draws pilgrims year-round — and a famous wave of devotees on New Year's Day, when lakhs climb the steps to begin the year with Murugan's blessings.

Because it sits so close to Tirupati, it pairs naturally with the Venkateswara pilgrimage — Vishnu on the Tirumala hill, and Murugan on the Tiruttani hill. This guide covers the significance, the steps and access, the timings, and how to reach from Tirupati.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many Tirupati trips. Adding Tiruttani gave my pilgrimage a different flavour — the Murugan tradition is powerful and distinct. Treat the details below as a guide and confirm timings locally, since they change.

<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — Tiruttani Murugan Temple:
<ul>
  <li>📍 Location: Tiruvallur district, Tamil Nadu — about 75 km from Tirupati (near the AP–TN border).</li>
  <li>🦚 Deity: Lord Murugan (Subramanya/Kartikeya) — one of the Arupadai Veedu (six sacred abodes).</li>
  <li>🪜 Access: a hilltop temple reached by about 365 steps (steps and road/transport options exist).</li>
  <li>🕕 Timings: roughly early morning to night, with ritual breaks (confirm locally).</li>
  <li>🚗 From Tirupati: ~1.5–2 hours by car/taxi or train to Tiruttani.</li>
  <li>🎉 New Year: a huge step-climbing rush on January 1.</li>
</ul></blockquote>

<h2>💡 Gap — why Tiruttani is so significant (Arupadai Veedu)</h2>
This is what sets Tiruttani apart from an ordinary temple. It is one of the <strong>Arupadai Veedu</strong> — the six holy abodes of Lord Murugan, the most sacred Murugan shrines in the tradition:
<ul>
  <li>The Arupadai Veedu are six temples, each marking a key event in Murugan's life</li>
  <li>Tiruttani is where, by tradition, Murugan <strong>rested after vanquishing the demon Surapadman</strong> — a place of peace and victory</li>
  <li>Visiting all six is a cherished pilgrimage for Murugan devotees; Tiruttani is the one closest to Tirupati</li>
  <li>The deity here is also strongly associated with granting wishes and removing obstacles</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If you're a Murugan devotee, the proximity to Tirupati is a gift — you can have darshan at one of the six sacred abodes during a Venkateswara trip. A Vishnu (Tirumala) + Murugan (Tiruttani) combination makes for a beautifully balanced pilgrimage.

<h2>About the temple</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Deity:</strong> Sri Subramanya Swamy (Murugan), the hilltop presiding deity</li>
  <li><strong>Setting:</strong> on the Tiruttani hill, reached by about 365 steps (or by road/transport to near the top)</li>
  <li><strong>Antiquity:</strong> an ancient temple with deep roots in Tamil Saiva-Kaumara tradition</li>
  <li><strong>Atmosphere:</strong> serene hilltop, with sweeping views and a distinct Tamil temple character</li>
</ul>

<h2>💡 Gap — the hilltop steps & access</h2>
<ul>
  <li>The temple sits atop the hill, traditionally reached by climbing about <strong>365 steps</strong> — one for each day of the year</li>
  <li>Many devotees climb the steps as part of the vow/experience</li>
  <li>There is also a <strong>road/transport option</strong> to reach near the top for those who cannot climb (elders, families with small children)</li>
  <li>The climb is moderate — wear comfortable footwear and carry water</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> If climbing the steps, go early morning or evening to avoid the midday heat, and pace yourself. For elders or those with mobility issues, use the road/vehicle access to the upper level instead of the steps.

<h2>Timings and darshan</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Open:</strong> roughly early morning to night, with midday/ritual breaks</li>
  <li><strong>Darshan:</strong> free general darshan; special darshan/sevas may be available for a fee</li>
  <li><strong>Wait:</strong> usually moderate; longer on Tamil festival days, Kartikai/Krittika days and New Year</li>
  <li><strong>Sevas:</strong> abhishekam and special poojas to Murugan can be performed — ask at the temple</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Timings, sevas and any darshan fees are set by the temple administration (in Tamil Nadu) and change — confirm before visiting. Krittika (Krithigai) days each month are especially significant and crowded for Murugan.

<h2>💡 Gap — the New Year step-climbing rush</h2>
Tiruttani is famous for an extraordinary scene on <strong>January 1</strong>: lakhs of devotees climb the steps to begin the new year with Murugan's blessings. It's one of the largest New Year temple gatherings in the region.
<ul>
  <li>The crowd is immense — plan for very long waits and heavy congestion</li>
  <li>If you want the New Year experience, prepare for the rush; otherwise avoid Jan 1 for a calm darshan</li>
  <li>Krittika days and Tamil festival times are also peak</li>
</ul>

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>

  <li>❌ <strong>Visiting on January 1 unprepared:</strong> the New Year step rush draws lakhs — expect huge crowds, or pick another day for a calm darshan.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Elders attempting the 365 steps:</strong> use the road/vehicle access to the upper level if climbing is difficult.</li>
