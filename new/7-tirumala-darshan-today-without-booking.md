You're already in Tirupati — or about to leave for it — and only now realise you never booked the darshan online. The ₹300 quota for your date is long gone, and panic sets in: "Can I even get darshan today without a booking?" The answer is yes, but the route matters a lot, and the wrong choice can cost you 20 hours.

Here is the reassuring truth: a confirmed online ticket is the easiest path, but it is not the only one. Several legitimate same-day, no-pre-booking options exist — free SSD tokens, the footpath Divya Darshan, a limited daily SRIVANI quota, and on-arrival categories for NRIs, infants, seniors and the differently-abled. Knowing them in the right order is the difference between a smooth darshan and a wasted day.

This guide ranks every no-booking option by how quick and reliable it is, tells you exactly where to go and what to carry, and gives a realistic same-day plan. It is the page to read the moment you find yourself without a ticket.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many Tirumala trips, including last-minute ones where I had to sort darshan on the ground. It is very doable with the right information. Treat the details below as a 2026 snapshot and confirm the live position on the official site and at the counters, since TTD adjusts tokens and quotas frequently.

<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — Darshan Today Without Booking:
<ul>
  <li>🆓 SSD (Slotted Sarva Darshan) token — free, from Tirupati counters; often a same/next-day slot.</li>
  <li>🚶 Divya Darshan — free token by walking up via Alipiri/Srivari Mettu (shorter wait).</li>
  <li>💎 SRIVANI VIP Break Darshan — a limited daily same-day online quota (~₹10,500).</li>
  <li>👶 On-arrival categories: NRI (Supadham), infant under 1, senior 65+, differently-abled, defence.</li>
  <li>🎟️ ₹300 Special Entry — online only; sometimes a current-booking quota opens — keep checking.</li>
  <li>⚠️ The non-token Sarva line is the last resort — it can run 20+ hours.</li>
</ul></blockquote>

<h2>First, set expectations</h2>
A same-day darshan without a pre-booked ticket is absolutely possible — TTD runs the temple 24x7 and serves over a lakh pilgrims daily. But "today" rarely means "in the next hour." Most no-booking routes involve a token and a wait, and the smart move is to pick the fastest route you qualify for, immediately, rather than drifting into the long free line.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Before anything, quickly check whether a <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">₹300 Special Entry</a> "current booking" slot has opened for today/tomorrow on the TTD portal — cancellations and current-quota releases do happen. If you find one, grab it; it's still the easiest path. Learn the checking method in <a href="/ttd-darshan-dates-kaise-check-karen/">how to check TTD dates</a>.

<h2>Option 1 — On-arrival categories (use first if you qualify)</h2>
If you fall into any of these, this is your fastest, cheapest route — no online booking needed:
<ul>
  <li><strong>NRI / foreign nationals:</strong> via the <a href="/tirumala-supadham-darshan-guide/">Supadham entrance</a> (passport, on-arrival, generally free)</li>
  <li><strong>Infant under 1 year:</strong> the <a href="/tirumala-darshan-with-baby/">infant fast-track</a> (birth certificate)</li>
  <li><strong>Senior citizens 65+:</strong> the <a href="/senior-citizen-tirumala-darshan/">senior darshan</a> route</li>
  <li><strong>Differently-abled:</strong> the <a href="/tirumala-wheelchair-darshan-guide/">physically-challenged darshan</a> (medical certificate)</li>
  <li><strong>Defence personnel:</strong> a dedicated provision with service ID</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> These categories exist precisely so the vulnerable and the visiting don't suffer the long line. If even one member of your group qualifies, ask staff how the accompanying family is handled — it can simplify the whole group's darshan.

<h2>Option 2 — SSD token (free, the main no-booking route)</h2>
For most resident Indians without a ticket, the Slotted Sarva Darshan (SSD) token is the primary free route.
<ul>
  <li><strong>Where:</strong> token-issuing counters in Tirupati — Srinivasam, Vishnu Nivasam, Bhudevi Complex (and as notified)</li>
  <li><strong>How:</strong> Aadhaar/biometric verification, free, first-come-first-served</li>
  <li><strong>Slot:</strong> the token carries a reporting time — often for later the same day or the next day</li>
  <li><strong>Wait with token:</strong> several hours, but far better than the non-token line</li>
</ul>
Understand it fully in the <a href="/tirumala-darshan-token-system/">darshan token system guide</a> and the <a href="/sarva-darshan-guide/">Sarva Darshan guide</a>.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Collect the SSD token early in the day — they're limited and a popular slot can run out. Carry Aadhaar for every person. If the same-day quota is exhausted, you may get a next-day slot, so factor that into your stay.

<h2>Option 3 — Divya Darshan (free, if you can walk up)</h2>
If you are reasonably fit, walking up the hill earns a free priority token:
<ul>
  <li>Walk up via the <a href="/alipiri-footpath-trek-guide/">Alipiri footpath</a> (~7.8 km) or <a href="/srivari-mettu-footpath-guide/">Srivari Mettu</a> (~2 km, steeper)</li>
  <li>Collect the Divya Darshan token on reaching Tirumala</li>
  <li>Wait is typically 2–4 hours — much shorter than the regular free line</li>
  <li>Free, no advance booking — your effort is the "ticket"</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Start the climb very early (2–4 AM) to reach by 6–8 AM and get a good Divya Darshan slot. It is the best free option for the fit — you save money and skip the worst of the queue. Tokens are limited, so early arrival matters.

<h2>Option 4 — SRIVANI same-day online quota (paid, fast)</h2>
If budget allows and you want a quick darshan today:
<ul>
  <li>A limited number of same-day SRIVANI tickets are released daily through the online current-booking system</li>
  <li>Cost ~₹10,500 (₹10,000 donation + ₹500 ticket), wait ~1–2 hours</li>
  <li>Some SRIVANI tickets are also issued at designated points (e.g. the JEO office on Tirumala / Tirupati Airport) — confirm current availability</li>
</ul>
Full details and the rush-day caveat: <a href="/srivani-trust-vip-break-darshan/">SRIVANI VIP Break Darshan guide</a>.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> The daily SRIVANI same-day quota is small and is cancelled on heavy-rush days. Don't count on it alone — keep an SSD token as backup.

<h2>Option 5 — Non-token Sarva Darshan (last resort)</h2>
The plain free line, without a slotted token, still exists — but it is the slowest:
<ul>
  <li>Wait can be 10–20+ hours on busy days</li>
  <li>You wait through the <a href="/vqc-vaikuntam-queue-complex-guide/">VQC</a> compartments</li>
  <li>Suitable only if all token/quota options are exhausted and you have lots of time</li>
</ul>

<h2>💡 Gap — a realistic same-day plan</h2>
Here's the order to work through the moment you find yourself ticketless:
<ol>
  <li><strong>Check the TTD portal</strong> for a ₹300 current-booking slot for today/tomorrow — grab it if available.</li>
  <li><strong>Do you qualify for an on-arrival category?</strong> (NRI/infant/senior/PH/defence) — if yes, use it; it's the fastest.</li>
  <li><strong>Fit enough to walk?</strong> Climb via Srivari Mettu/Alipiri for a free Divya Darshan token.</li>
  <li><strong>Want to pay for speed?</strong> Try the SRIVANI same-day quota.</li>
  <li><strong>Otherwise,</strong> collect an SSD token from a Tirupati counter early in the day.</li>
  <li><strong>Last resort:</strong> the non-token Sarva line, only if you have the hours.</li>
</ol>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Whatever route you pick, sort accommodation in parallel — a same/next-day token can mean an overnight stay. See <a href="/tirumala-stay-options-pilgrims/">Tirumala stay options</a>. And remember: only the official TTD portal and counters are genuine — never pay an agent promising "instant darshan today."

<h2>Dress code applies to every route</h2>
No matter which no-booking option you use, the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">dress code</a> is enforced — traditional attire, phones into the locker. A token or on-arrival category does not exempt you. To understand how all darshan types relate, read <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">types of Tirumala darshan</a>.

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Drifting straight into the long free line:</strong> first check for a ₹300 current slot, an on-arrival category, or a Divya/SSD token — the non-token line should be the last resort, not the first.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Collecting the SSD token late:</strong> tokens are limited and good slots run out — get to the Tirupati counter early in the day.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Banking only on the SRIVANI same-day quota:</strong> it's small and cancelled in heavy rush. Keep an SSD token as backup.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Forgetting Aadhaar for everyone:</strong> SSD tokens and most categories need ID for each person — carry originals.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Paying an agent for "instant darshan":</strong> there is no legitimate paid shortcut beyond SRIVANI/₹300 — agents are a scam.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not planning for a next-day slot:</strong> same-day tokens can run out, giving you a next-day reporting time — arrange a stay accordingly.</li>
</ul>
