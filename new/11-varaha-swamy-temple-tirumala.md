Most pilgrims rush straight to Lord Venkateswara, have their darshan, collect the laddu and leave — never realising they skipped the temple that tradition says should come <strong>first</strong>. Right beside the Swami Pushkarini, a short walk from the main shrine, stands the Sri Varaha Swamy temple — and by long-standing custom, the Lord here is to be worshipped before Venkateswara.

The reason is rooted in the sthala purana (the temple's legend): Sri Varaha Swamy — the boar incarnation of Lord Vishnu — was the original lord of the Venkatadri hill. When Lord Venkateswara wished to reside here, He took the land from Varaha Swamy, and in return granted Him the honour of being worshipped first, with the first naivedyam (food offering) too. That custom continues to this day.

Yet, because nobody points it out, lakhs of pilgrims walk right past it. This guide explains the legend, why you should visit first, where the temple is, the timings, and how to fit it neatly into your darshan plan.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many Tirumala trips. On my early visits I missed Varaha Swamy too; once I understood the tradition, the darshan felt more complete. Treat the details below as a guide and confirm timings on the official site, since they vary.

  <li>📍 Location: on the bank of the Swami Pushkarini, a short walk from the main temple.</li>

<h2>💡 Gap — the legend of the land (why visit first)</h2>
This is the story almost no guide tells clearly. According to the sthala purana:
<ul>
  <li>The Venkatadri hill was originally the abode of <strong>Sri Varaha Swamy</strong> (Adi Varaha), the boar incarnation of Vishnu who once lifted the earth from the cosmic ocean.</li>
  <li>When Lord Venkateswara (Srinivasa) chose to reside on this hill, He requested land from Varaha Swamy.</li>
  <li>Varaha Swamy granted the land, and in return Venkateswara decreed that <strong>Varaha Swamy would be worshipped first</strong> — receiving the first darshan and the first naivedyam.</li>
  <li>To honour this, tradition holds that a pilgrim's worship at Tirumala should begin with Varaha Swamy before proceeding to Venkateswara.</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> This is the same "land grant" theme that runs through Tirumala's legends — including the famous story of Venkateswara's loan from Kubera. Knowing the Varaha Swamy connection makes the whole hill's mythology click into place. For the wider story, read the <a href="/tirumala-mandir-itihas/">Tirumala temple history</a>.

<h2>About the temple</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Deity:</strong> Sri Varaha Swamy (Adi Varaha) — depicted as the boar-faced incarnation</li>
  <li><strong>Antiquity:</strong> considered one of the oldest shrines on the hill — older, by tradition, than the main temple's prominence</li>
  <li><strong>Setting:</strong> on the north-western bank of the sacred Swami Pushkarini tank — a serene spot</li>
  <li><strong>Atmosphere:</strong> calmer and quieter than the main temple, with a much shorter queue</li>
</ul>

<h2>💡 Gap — the first-naivedyam custom</h2>
A unique living tradition: the <strong>first food offering (naivedyam)</strong> at Tirumala is made to Sri Varaha Swamy, not to Venkateswara. This daily honour reflects the legend's promise. It is a small but profound detail — the "landlord" of the hill is served first each day, a model of gratitude and dharma that pilgrims are encouraged to mirror by taking His darshan first.

<h2>Location — where is the Varaha Swamy temple?</h2>
<ul>
  <li>On the bank of the <strong>Swami Pushkarini</strong>, the sacred tank right beside the main Venkateswara temple</li>
  <li>A short walk (a few minutes) from the main temple complex</li>
  <li>Easy to reach on foot; ask any staff member for "Varaha Swamy temple" and they'll point the way</li>
  <li>Many pilgrims take a holy dip/sprinkle at the Pushkarini before darshan</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Because it sits right by the Swami Pushkarini, you can combine a quiet moment at the tank with the Varaha Swamy darshan — a peaceful start before the intensity of the main temple crowd.

<h2>Timings and darshan</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Darshan:</strong> free, with a separate (usually short) queue</li>
  <li><strong>Timings:</strong> broadly aligned with the main temple's open hours, with its own ritual schedule — confirm on the day</li>
  <li><strong>Wait:</strong> typically far shorter than the main Venkateswara darshan</li>
  <li><strong>Duration:</strong> a quick darshan — easy to do in addition to your main darshan</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Exact Varaha Swamy temple timings and any ritual closures are set by TTD and can vary — confirm on <a href="https://tirumala.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">tirumala.org</a> or at the Tirumala enquiry desk. For the main temple's full schedule, see the <a href="/tirumala-temple-timings/">Tirumala temple timings guide</a>.

<h2>How to fit it into your darshan plan</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Ideally:</strong> Varaha Swamy darshan first → then the main <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">Venkateswara darshan</a></li>
  <li><strong>Practically:</strong> if your Venkateswara slot timing is tight, many pilgrims do Varaha Swamy right after the main darshan instead — it is still meaningful</li>
  <li><strong>With a Pushkarini visit:</strong> combine the tank and Varaha Swamy as one peaceful sequence</li>
  <li><strong>Time needed:</strong> add roughly 30–45 minutes for Varaha Swamy darshan</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If you're staying overnight on the hill, do the Varaha Swamy darshan in the calm early morning or evening, separate from your main darshan slot — you'll have it almost to yourself, and the tradition is honoured without any rush.

<h2>Dress code and conduct</h2>
The temple <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">dress code</a> applies here as everywhere on Tirumala — traditional attire, phones deposited. The shorter queue does not change the rules. Maintain silence and reverence as in the main temple.

<h2>Why pilgrims should not skip it</h2>
<ul>
  <li>It honours a living tradition central to Tirumala's own legend</li>
  <li>The darshan is quick and free — minimal extra time for a complete pilgrimage</li>
  <li>The setting by the Swami Pushkarini is serene — a calm contrast to the main crowd</li>
  <li>Many devotees feel their Tirumala visit is incomplete without it</li>
</ul>

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Skipping it entirely:</strong> the most common miss — pilgrims don't know the tradition. It's a few minutes' walk and a short darshan; don't leave without it.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not knowing the visit-first custom:</strong> tradition places Varaha Swamy before Venkateswara. Do it first if your timing allows.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Assuming it needs a separate ticket:</strong> the Varaha Swamy darshan is free — no special booking required.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Rushing past the Pushkarini:</strong> the temple sits right by the sacred tank — take a quiet moment there too.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Treating the dress code as relaxed:</strong> the same Tirumala dress and conduct rules apply.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Leaving no time for it:</strong> build ~30–45 minutes into your plan so you don't have to skip it when the schedule tightens.</li>
</ul>
