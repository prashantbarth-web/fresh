A one-day Tirupati trip is a rushed dash, and even two days can feel tight once you add the nearby temples. Three days is the sweet spot — enough to have an unhurried Tirumala darshan and cover the sacred circle around Tirupati: Padmavathi at Tiruchanur, Shiva at Sri Kalahasti, Ganesha at Kanipakam, and Kapila Theertham. It turns a single darshan into a complete pilgrimage.

The catch most pilgrims miss is geography. These temples lie on different sides of Tirupati — Tiruchanur and Kapila Theertham are close, Kalahasti is to the north-east, and Kanipakam is toward Chittoor. Plan the order wrong and you waste hours backtracking. Plan it right and three days flow beautifully.

This guide gives you a clean day-by-day 3-day itinerary, the route logic, what to pre-book, and the budget and stay tips — so you cover everything without rushing or doubling back.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many Tirumala trips, several stretched to three days for exactly this circuit. The extra day is what makes the trip feel complete rather than frantic. Treat the plan below as a template and confirm timings/tickets on the official sites before you go.

<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — Tirupati 3-Day Itinerary:
<ul>
  <li>📅 Day 1: Arrive Tirupati → Kapila Theertham → up to Tirumala (overnight).</li>
  <li>📅 Day 2: Tirumala darshan + laddu → down to Tirupati → Tiruchanur Padmavathi temple.</li>
  <li>📅 Day 3: Sri Kalahasti (and/or Kanipakam) → depart.</li>
  <li>🎟️ Pre-book: the ₹300 SED darshan and Tirumala/Tirupati stay.</li>
  <li>🧭 Route logic: group nearby temples; don't criss-cross Tirupati.</li>
  <li>💰 Budget: roughly ₹6,000–12,000 per person for 3 days (travel extra).</li>
</ul></blockquote>

<h2>Why three days is ideal</h2>
<ul>
  <li>An unhurried Tirumala darshan with a night's stay on the hill (better than a same-day dash)</li>
  <li>Time for the full sacred circle — Vishnu (Tirumala), Padmavathi (Tiruchanur), Shiva (Kalahasti), Ganesha (Kanipakam)</li>
  <li>Buffer for queue delays, so one slow darshan doesn't wreck the plan</li>
  <li>A relaxed pace for families with elders and children</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If you only have two days, do the <a href="/tirupati-2-day-itinerary/">2-day itinerary</a> (Tirumala + Tiruchanur). If you have just one, see the <a href="/tirupati-1-day-darshan/">1-day darshan plan</a> — but three days is what lets you cover the nearby temples without rushing.

<h2>💡 Gap — route logic (the key to a smooth 3 days)</h2>
The temples sit on different sides of Tirupati, so group them:
<ul>
  <li><strong>Close to Tirupati city:</strong> Tiruchanur (~5 km), Kapila Theertham (~2 km), Govindaraja Swamy (in town)</li>
  <li><strong>North-east:</strong> Sri Kalahasti (~36 km)</li>
  <li><strong>Toward Chittoor (west):</strong> Kanipakam (~68–70 km)</li>
  <li><strong>On the hill:</strong> Tirumala (~22 km up)</li>
</ul>
Because Kalahasti and Kanipakam are on opposite sides, don't try to do both in one day by bus — use a cab, or split them across the two non-Tirumala half-days.

<h2>Day 1 — Arrival + up to Tirumala</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Morning:</strong> reach Tirupati, check in, freshen up</li>
  <li><strong>Late morning:</strong> Govindaraja Swamy temple (in town) and/or <a href="/kapila-theertham-tirupati/">Kapila Theertham</a> (the Shiva temple + waterfall near Alipiri)</li>
  <li><strong>Afternoon:</strong> take the <a href="/tirupati-se-tirumala-kaise-jayen/">TTD bus up to Tirumala</a> (~22 km)</li>
  <li><strong>Evening:</strong> settle into your <a href="/tirumala-stay-options-pilgrims/">Tirumala accommodation</a>; visit the Swami Pushkarini and Varaha Swamy temple; <a href="/tirumala-annadanam-guide/">Annadanam</a> dinner</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Staying on the hill on Day 1 night sets up an early, calmer Day 2 darshan and lets you soak in the temple-town atmosphere overnight.

<h2>Day 2 — Tirumala darshan + Tiruchanur</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Early morning:</strong> Tirumala darshan with your pre-booked <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">₹300 SED ticket</a> (2–4 hour wait vs 10+ for free)</li>
  <li><strong>After darshan:</strong> collect <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">laddu prasadam</a>; a quick look around the temple precinct</li>
  <li><strong>Late morning:</strong> bus/cab down to Tirupati</li>
  <li><strong>Afternoon:</strong> <a href="/tiruchanur-padmavathi-temple-guide/">Tiruchanur Padmavathi temple</a> (~5 km) — tradition says complete the pilgrimage with Padmavathi Devi's darshan</li>
  <li><strong>Evening:</strong> rest in Tirupati; optional local market/silk shopping</li>
</ul>

<h2>Day 3 — Sri Kalahasti and/or Kanipakam</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Option A (north-east):</strong> <a href="/sri-kalahasti-temple-guide/">Sri Kalahasti</a> (~36 km) — the Vayu Lingam Shiva temple, famous for the Rahu-Ketu puja</li>
  <li><strong>Option B (west):</strong> <a href="/kanipakam-vinayaka-temple-guide/">Kanipakam</a> (~68–70 km) — the self-growing Ganesha</li>
  <li><strong>Both in a day:</strong> only with an early start and a private cab (they're on opposite sides)</li>
  <li><strong>Then:</strong> return to Tirupati and depart by train/flight</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If you can only fit one on Day 3, choose by devotion — Kalahasti for a Shiva/Rahu-Ketu remedy, Kanipakam for Ganesha. To do both, hire a full-day cab and start by 5:30–6 AM.

<h2>Tips for a smooth 3 days</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Pre-book the SED ticket for Day 2 morning — the backbone of the plan</li>
  <li>Keep Day 3 flexible — weather/queues can shift timings</li>
  <li>Carry traditional dress for all temple darshans (<a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">dress code</a>)</li>
  <li>A full-day cab on Day 3 saves the most time if combining temples</li>
  <li>Don't over-pack the schedule — leave room to rest, especially with elders</li>
</ul>

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Doing Kalahasti and Kanipakam by bus in one day:</strong> they're on opposite sides of Tirupati — use a cab, or split them.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not staying overnight on Tirumala:</strong> the Day 1 hill stay makes Day 2 darshan calmer and earlier.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Skipping the SED booking:</strong> without it, Day 2 can collapse into a 10+ hour free-line wait.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Over-scheduling:</strong> trying to add too many temples turns the trip frantic — three days is for an unhurried circuit.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ignoring temple-side geography:</strong> plan nearby temples by area to avoid backtracking.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Leaving the return open:</strong> book the return train/flight early.</li>
</ul>
