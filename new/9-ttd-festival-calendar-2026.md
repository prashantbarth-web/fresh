Two pilgrims plan a Tirumala trip for the same week — one lands in the middle of a spectacular festival with lakhs of devotees and a 20-hour queue, the other gets a calm darshan in two hours. The only difference? One checked the festival calendar; the other didn't.

The Tirumala temple runs a packed festival calendar through the year — Brahmotsavam, Vaikunta Ekadasi, Ratha Saptami, Vasanthotsavam and many more. Each is a magnificent spiritual occasion, but each also multiplies the crowd. For planning, the festival calendar is two tools in one: a list of experiences to seek out, and a list of dates to avoid if you want a quick, quiet darshan.

And 2026 is special. Because of an Adhika Masa (an extra lunar month), Tirumala celebrates not one but TWO Brahmotsavams this year — a rare event. This guide gives you the full-year, month-by-month calendar, flags which festivals to plan around versus avoid, and shows you how to confirm the exact dates.

One honest caveat: festival dates follow the Telugu Panchangam and shift each year, and TTD announces them officially — so treat the months below as a planning guide and confirm exact dates on the official site.

<h2>💡 Gap — why 2026 has two Brahmotsavams</h2>
The headline event of the year. In the Hindu lunar calendar, an Adhika Masa (an extra month) is inserted periodically to realign the lunar and solar years — and 2026 is such a year.

Because of it, Tirumala holds the grand festival twice:
<ul>
  <li><strong>Salakatla Brahmotsavam</strong> — the annual Brahmotsavam, in September 2026</li>
  <li><strong>Navaratri Brahmotsavam</strong> — the additional one, in October 2026, aligned with Navaratri</li>
</ul>
Both are full 9-day festivals with the complete vahana seva sequence — a genuinely rare opportunity for devotees. Full details: <a href="/brahmotsavam-2026-dates-schedule/">Brahmotsavam 2026 dates and schedule</a>.

<h2>The big four — what to know</h2>

<h3>1. Brahmotsavam (Sept & Oct 2026)</h3>
Tirumala's grandest festival — 9 days of daily vahana processions (Garuda Vahanam, Rathotsavam and more). In 2026 it happens twice. Plan 2–3 months ahead, and expect 3–5 lakh daily. Full guide: <a href="/brahmotsavam-2026-dates-schedule/">Brahmotsavam 2026</a>.

<h3>2. Vaikunta Ekadasi (~late December 2026)</h3>
On this day the <strong>Vaikunta Dwaram</strong> (the gateway to the Lord's heavenly abode) is opened, and devotees pass through it — believed to be exceptionally auspicious. The darshan runs over multiple days. Crowds are extreme (often managed via online e-dip tokens), and it is the busiest period of the year.

<h3>3. Ratha Saptami (~late Jan / early Feb 2026)</h3>
A single day on which the Lord is taken out on <strong>all seven vahanas</strong> across the day — effectively a one-day Brahmotsavam. Importantly, this is a free, open spectacle you can watch without a darshan ticket. A favourite for those who want the Brahmotsavam feel in a single day. Full guide: <a href="/ratha-saptami-tirumala-guide/">Ratha Saptami Tirumala</a>.

<h3>4. Vasanthotsavam (spring)</h3>
A three-day spring festival with the deities and special rituals, in the pleasant March–April window — beautiful and comparatively less overwhelming than Brahmotsavam.

<h2>How festivals affect darshan and crowds</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Wait times multiply:</strong> the free line can hit 20+ hours; even ₹300 slots vanish months ahead — see <a href="/tirumala-darshan-waiting-time/">waiting time</a></li>
  <li><strong>Darshan starts later:</strong> on festival days many rituals are done in Ekantam, delaying general darshan</li>
  <li><strong>Accommodation fills early:</strong> book Tirumala/Tirupati stays 2–3 months ahead for festival dates</li>
  <li><strong>Premium darshan may be suspended:</strong> SRIVANI/VIP break darshan is often cancelled in heavy rush</li>
  <li><strong>Free processions:</strong> the upside — vahana sevas are open to watch without a ticket</li>
</ul>
To understand the darshan types around festivals, read <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">types of Tirumala darshan</a> and the <a href="/ttd-arjitha-sevas-list/">Arjitha Sevas list</a>.

<h2>💡 Gap — how to confirm the exact 2026 dates</h2>
Because Panchangam dates shift, confirm before booking:
<ul>
  <li>Check tirumala.org → "Sevas & Darshans" / "Annual Events"</li>
  <li>Follow the official TTD social handle — it announces festival dates 2–3 months ahead</li>
  <li>For the monthly online quota dates (darshan/seva/accommodation), watch TTD's monthly release notices</li>
  <li>Do not lock flights/trains to a festival date you read on a blog — verify officially first</li>
</ul>

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Booking travel on a festival date unknowingly:</strong> a normal trip landing mid-Brahmotsavam or on Vaikunta Ekadasi means no ₹300 ticket and a brutal queue. Check the calendar first.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Trusting blog dates as final:</strong> Panchangam dates vary; only TTD's official announcement is authoritative.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Last-minute festival plans:</strong> Brahmotsavam and Vaikunta Ekadasi need 2–3 months of advance booking for tickets and stay.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Expecting a quick darshan during a festival:</strong> waits are extreme; plan 3+ days, or watch the free processions instead.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Missing the two-Brahmotsavam opportunity:</strong> 2026's Adhika Masa gives a rare second Brahmotsavam in October — a special chance for devotees.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ignoring the post-festival lull:</strong> February and November offer great weather and lighter crowds — underrated windows.</li>
</ul>
