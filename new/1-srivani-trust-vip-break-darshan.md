For years, a fast "VIP" darshan at Tirumala felt like something only VIPs, politicians or people with "recommendation letters" could get. Ordinary pilgrims stood in the 10–20 hour free line and watched others walk in through a side gate. That changed with the SRIVANI Trust scheme — and it is the single most misunderstood darshan option at Tirumala today.

Here is the simple idea: through the SRIVANI (Sri Venkateswara Aalayala Nirmanam) Trust, you make a donation towards building and maintaining Venkateswara temples across India, and in return you become eligible for a VIP Break Darshan — a far shorter, privileged entry. It is the legal, transparent route to a quick darshan that any member of the public can use.

But there are real catches that competitor pages gloss over: the true total cost is more than the "₹10,000" figure you see everywhere, the online quota sells out fast, and — most importantly — TTD cancels SRIVANI and VIP Break Darshan entirely on heavy-rush days. Booking without knowing this leads to disappointment.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many trips to Tirumala over the years. I have done both the long free queue and the quicker paid routes, and for a family with elders on a tight schedule, the time saved is genuinely valuable. This guide gives you the honest, current picture. Treat the figures as a 2026 snapshot and confirm the live rules on the official site before paying — TTD revises them often.

<h2>What is the SRIVANI Trust scheme?</h2>
SRIVANI stands for the <strong>Sri Venkateswara Aalayala Nirmanam Trust</strong> — a TTD trust whose funds are used to build and maintain Sri Venkateswara temples across India.

The scheme links a donation to a privileged darshan. When you donate to SRIVANI (a minimum amount), you become eligible to book a <strong>VIP Break Darshan</strong> — the kind of quicker, priority entry that was earlier hard for the general public to access.

In short: it converted the old, opaque "VIP/recommendation" darshan into a transparent, donation-based system open to everyone. Your money goes to temple construction; you get a faster darshan in return.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Think of SRIVANI as a donation that comes with a darshan privilege — not as "buying a ticket." Framing it that way also sets the right expectation: it is premium-priced because it is fundamentally a contribution to temple building, with the darshan as a benefit.

<h2>How to book SRIVANI Trust darshan — step by step</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Go to the official TTD portal (ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in / tirumala.org) and log in (or register).</li>
  <li>Open the "SRIVANI Trust" / "SRIVANI Darshan" section.</li>
  <li>Select your date — both an advance quota and a limited same-day online quota exist (see below).</li>
  <li>Enter each pilgrim's name and photo-ID details.</li>
  <li>Pay the donation + ticket amount online (UPI/card/net banking).</li>
  <li>Download and save the SRIVANI darshan confirmation/ePass — carry a printout.</li>
</ol>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> SRIVANI darshan tickets are released online on TTD's announced schedule — for example, as part of the monthly online quota release — and a limited number of same-day tickets are also issued through the online current-booking system. Both sell out quickly. Book only on the official portal; never through an agent or a third-party "SRIVANI booking" site.

<h3>Reporting and entry</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Reach the designated reporting point at the time on your ePass.</li>
  <li>Carry the original photo ID matching the booking, plus the donation receipt/ePass.</li>
  <li>Entry is generally via the privileged/break-darshan route, which is why the wait is short.</li>
  <li>The dress code applies exactly as for any darshan — see the rules below.</li>
</ul>

<h2>💡 Gap — the rush-day cancellation catch</h2>
This is the most important thing competitor pages bury. On heavy-rush days, <strong>TTD cancels SRIVANI and VIP Break Darshan entirely</strong> to prioritise common pilgrims — including the daily same-day online SRIVANI quota.

This has happened repeatedly in 2026 during peak periods (long weekends, summer holidays, festival rushes). So:
<ul>
  <li>SRIVANI is <strong>not a guaranteed</strong> fast-track on every date.</li>
  <li>During May–July peaks and festival days, it may be suspended at short notice.</li>
  <li>Recommendation-letter (protocol) VIP darshan has also been suspended during such rushes.</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Do not build your entire trip around SRIVANI on a peak date. Always keep a backup — a <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">₹300 Special Entry Darshan</a> slot or, worst case, the free <a href="/tirumala-darshan-waiting-time/">Sarva Darshan queue</a>. Check the latest advisory on tirumala.org and the official TTD handle a day or two before travel.

<h2>💡 Gap — who is SRIVANI actually worth it for?</h2>
<ul>
  <li>✅ <strong>Worth it:</strong> families with elderly members who cannot stand for hours; a strict 24-hour visit where every hour counts; dates when the ₹300 slot is already sold out; devotees who want to contribute to temple construction anyway.</li>
  <li>⚠️ <strong>Think twice:</strong> budget travellers (the ₹300 darshan saves almost as much time at a fraction of the cost); large groups (₹10,500 per head adds up fast); peak-festival dates when it may be cancelled.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not needed:</strong> off-season weekdays, when even the ₹300 wait is short and the free line is manageable.</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> On a past summer trip with my parents, the ₹300 slots were gone and the free line was 18+ hours — SRIVANI was genuinely the right call for the elders. On an off-season weekday, I'd never spend it; the ₹300 darshan is enough. Match the option to the situation, not to status.

<h2>What's included with SRIVANI darshan</h2>
<ul>
  <li>VIP Break Darshan entry (the short-wait route)</li>
  <li>The satisfaction of a temple-construction donation, with an official receipt</li>
  <li>Laddu prasadam after darshan, as per the current norm — see the <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">laddu guide</a></li>
</ul>
Note: accommodation, transport and sevas are separate — SRIVANI covers the darshan privilege only.

<h2>Dress code and rules</h2>
The dress code is enforced for SRIVANI darshan just like any other:
<ul>
  <li>✅ Men: dhoti/kurta or formal traditional wear</li>
  <li>✅ Women: saree or salwar-kameez</li>
  <li>❌ Jeans, shorts, sleeveless — refused at the gate</li>
  <li>📱 Phones go into the locker before the queue</li>
</ul>
The full picture is in the <a href="/ttd-darshan-rules-2026/">TTD darshan rules 2026</a>.

<h2>SRIVANI for NRIs and foreign devotees</h2>
NRIs and foreign nationals can use SRIVANI too, paying through the online portal. That said, NRIs often have a dedicated faster route via the Supadham/NRI darshan — compare both in the <a href="/nri-darshan-guide/">NRI darshan guide</a> before deciding, since the NRI route may be cheaper and equally quick.

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Assuming SRIVANI guarantees darshan on any date:</strong> it is cancelled on heavy-rush days. Always keep a ₹300 or free-line backup.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Booking through an agent or third-party site:</strong> only the official TTD portal is genuine. Agents charging extra "SRIVANI packages" are a fraud risk — report brokers on the TTD toll-free 155257.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Thinking ₹10,000 covers the family:</strong> the donation is generally per person for the darshan benefit. Calculate the full per-head cost for a group.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Spending on SRIVANI when the ₹300 slot is available:</strong> on a normal day the ₹300 Special Entry Darshan saves almost the same time for far less.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ignoring the dress code:</strong> SRIVANI is no exception — wrong dress means no entry, money or not.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not saving the receipt/ePass:</strong> you need the donation receipt and matching original ID at reporting; keep a printout and a screenshot.</li>
</ul>
