Every pilgrim drops something into the Hundi at Tirumala — but far fewer know that you can contribute to the Lord's work from home, choose exactly what your money supports, and get a tax benefit for it. TTD runs a transparent online donation system: an e-Hundi for general offerings, plus a set of trusts funding everything from free meals and free heart surgeries to temple construction and cow protection.

This matters for two kinds of devotees — those who want to give back meaningfully (and know their money is well-used), and those exploring schemes like SRIVANI that pair a donation with a darshan privilege. Understanding the options helps you give in the way that fits your intention.

This guide covers the main TTD donation trusts, how to donate online step by step, the 80G tax benefit, and the honest truth about donor privileges — what they include and what they don't.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many Tirumala trips. Contributing to a cause like Annadanam, knowing it feeds thousands daily, adds a quiet dimension to the pilgrimage. Treat the scheme details below as a guide and confirm current amounts and privileges on the official site, since TTD revises them.

<h2>What is the e-Hundi?</h2>
The Hundi is the temple's donation box, into which devotees offer money, gold and more. The <strong>e-Hundi</strong> is its online version — you can make an offering to the Lord from anywhere, of any amount, through the official TTD portal.
<ul>
  <li>A general offering (not tied to a specific cause)</li>
  <li>Any amount you wish</li>
  <li>An instant online receipt</li>
  <li>The simplest way to "offer to the Hundi" without being at the temple</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If you simply want to make an offering in gratitude — for a wish fulfilled, a safe journey, an occasion — the e-Hundi is the easiest route. For a specific cause (meals, health, temple-building), choose the matching trust below.

<h2>💡 Gap — how to donate online (step by step)</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Go to the official TTD portal (tirumala.org / ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in) → the "Donations" / "e-Hundi" section</li>
  <li>Choose e-Hundi (general) or a specific trust/scheme</li>
  <li>Enter your details — name, PAN (for tax receipt), address, contact</li>
  <li>Enter the donation amount</li>
  <li>Pay online (UPI / card / net banking)</li>
  <li>Download/save the official receipt — keep it for tax and records</li>
</ol>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Enter your PAN correctly so the receipt is valid for the 80G tax claim. Save both the on-screen confirmation and the emailed receipt.

<h2>The 80G tax benefit</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Donations to TTD trusts generally qualify for income-tax exemption under Section 80G</li>
  <li>The official receipt (with the trust details and your PAN) is what you use to claim it</li>
  <li>The exact percentage/limit depends on the scheme and current tax rules</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Tax-exemption applicability and limits depend on the specific scheme and prevailing rules. Verify the 80G eligibility shown on the receipt, and consult a tax advisor for your specific case — this guide is informational, not tax advice.

<h2>💡 Gap — donor privileges (and what they aren't)</h2>
Some schemes extend privileges to donors above certain amounts — for example, darshan or accommodation benefits:
<ul>
  <li><strong>SRIVANI:</strong> a ₹10,000 donation makes you eligible for a VIP Break Darshan (plus a ₹500 ticket) — see the <a href="/srivani-trust-vip-break-darshan/">SRIVANI guide</a></li>
  <li><strong>Annaprasadam / other trusts:</strong> larger donations may carry darshan/accommodation privileges as per the scheme rules</li>
  <li>Donors often receive an acknowledgment certificate and, for some schemes, recognition</li>
</ul>

<strong>What privileges are NOT:</strong>
<ul>
  <li>A small e-Hundi offering does not automatically grant special darshan</li>
  <li>Privileges are scheme- and amount-specific, set by TTD, and can be suspended in heavy rush (as SRIVANI/VIP darshan sometimes is)</li>
  <li>No agent can "arrange" donor privileges — only the official portal is genuine</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Donate for the cause first, and treat any privilege as secondary. If your main goal is a fast darshan, weigh SRIVANI against the simpler <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">₹300 Special Entry Darshan</a> rather than donating purely for the privilege.

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Donating through a third-party site/agent:</strong> only the official TTD portal is genuine — others risk fraud.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Forgetting to enter PAN:</strong> without correct PAN details, the 80G receipt may not be valid for a tax claim.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Expecting darshan from a small donation:</strong> privileges are scheme- and amount-specific; a small e-Hundi offering doesn't grant special darshan.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Donating purely for a fast darshan:</strong> if speed is the only goal, the ₹300 SED ticket is simpler; donate for the cause.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not saving the receipt:</strong> keep the on-screen and emailed receipt for records and tax.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Assuming privileges are guaranteed always:</strong> donor darshan privileges (like SRIVANI) can be suspended on heavy-rush days.</li>
</ul>
