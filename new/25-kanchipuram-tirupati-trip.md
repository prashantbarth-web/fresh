Two of South India's holiest cities sit just a couple of hours apart — and pairing them makes one of the most rewarding pilgrim circuits in the country. Tirupati, the abode of Lord Venkateswara, and Kanchipuram, the ancient "city of a thousand temples" and one of the seven Moksha-puris (cities that grant liberation). Doing both in one trip means Vishnu, Shiva and Shakti darshan across a single, beautifully connected journey.

Kanchipuram is roughly 120 km from Tirupati and about 75 km from Chennai, which is why the classic route is Chennai → Kanchipuram → Tirupati (or the reverse). It's a natural circuit, especially if you're flying into or out of Chennai.

This guide covers the Kanchipuram temples worth your time, the best route order, the distances and transport, and a combined itinerary — so you experience both cities without backtracking or rushing.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many Tirumala trips, several combined with Kanchipuram. The shift from Kanchipuram's ancient stone temples to Tirumala's living grandeur is something special. Treat the details below as a guide and confirm temple timings locally, since they vary.

  <li>📍 Distance: Kanchipuram ↔ Tirupati ~120 km (~2.5–3 hours); Kanchipuram ↔ Chennai ~75 km.</li>
  <li>🛕 Kanchipuram highlights: Kamakshi Amman, Ekambareswarar, Varadaraja Perumal, Kailasanathar.</li>
  <li>🧭 Best route: Chennai → Kanchipuram → Tirupati (or reverse) — a no-backtrack circuit.</li>
  <li>🧵 Bonus: Kanchipuram is world-famous for its silk sarees.</li>
  <li>🗓️ Time needed: 3–4 days for a comfortable combined trip.</li>
  <li>🚗 Transport: train/bus on each leg, or a cab for the full circuit.</li>
</ul></blockquote>

<h2>Why combine Kanchipuram and Tirupati?</h2>
<ul>
  <li>They're close — about 120 km apart — so little extra travel for a whole new holy city</li>
  <li>Kanchipuram is one of the <strong>seven Moksha-puris</strong> and a rare city sacred to both Vaishnavites and Shaivites</li>
  <li>You cover <strong>Vishnu, Shiva and Shakti</strong> darshan in one circuit</li>
  <li>It fits naturally with a Chennai arrival/departure (Chennai–Kanchipuram is ~75 km)</li>
  <li>Kanchipuram silk shopping is a memorable bonus</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> If you're flying into Chennai for Tirupati anyway, adding Kanchipuram costs barely any detour — it's right on the way. See the <a href="/chennai-se-tirumala-kaise-jayen/">Chennai to Tirumala guide</a> for the Chennai leg.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Kanchipuram is divided into "Vishnu Kanchi" (Vaishnava temples like Varadaraja) and "Shiva Kanchi" (Shaiva temples like Ekambareswarar and Kailasanathar). Plan your temple order by area to avoid criss-crossing the town.

<h2>💡 Gap — the best route order</h2>
<table>
<tbody>
<tr><th>Route</th><th>When it works</th></tr>

The cleanest, no-backtrack circuit is <strong>Chennai → Kanchipuram → Tirupati</strong> — you move in one direction, ending with the grand finale at Tirumala.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> A Kanchipuram day trip from Tirupati (~120 km each way) means 5–6 hours of driving plus temple time — doable but tiring. Staying a night in Kanchipuram, or doing it as part of a Chennai-based leg, is far more comfortable.

<h2>Kanchipuram silk — the famous bonus</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Kanchipuram is world-renowned for its handwoven pure-silk sarees (Kanjeevaram)</li>
  <li>Buy from established cooperative/government showrooms or reputed weavers for authenticity</li>
  <li>Genuine Kanchipuram silk carries the GI tag and a silk mark — check before buying</li>
  <li>Keep 1–2 hours if you plan to shop; it's a highlight for many visitors</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> "Kanchipuram silk" is sold widely, but authentic handwoven pieces command a premium. Buy from trusted showrooms, look for the silk mark/GI assurance, and be wary of heavily discounted "pure silk."

<h2>Best time for the combined trip</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>October–February:</strong> the most pleasant weather for both cities</li>
  <li>Avoid peak summer (Mar–Jun) — Kanchipuram and the plains get very hot</li>
  <li>Mind major festivals at both ends (crowds) — see the <a href="/tirupati-best-time-to-visit/">best time to visit guide</a></li>
</ul>

<h2>Dress code and temple etiquette</h2>
<ul>
  <li>✅ Traditional/modest attire at all temples</li>
  <li>❌ Photography generally restricted inside sanctums</li>
  <li>✅ Footwear deposited; carry an ID</li>
  <li>Kanchipuram temples have their own timings and ritual breaks — plan around midday closures</li>
</ul>

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Trying to do Kanchipuram as a tight day trip from Tirupati:</strong> ~120 km each way plus temple time is exhausting — stay a night or do it on the Chennai leg.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Criss-crossing the town:</strong> plan Kanchipuram temples by area (Vishnu Kanchi vs Shiva Kanchi) to save time.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ignoring midday closures:</strong> Kanchipuram temples close for ritual breaks — schedule around them.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Buying "silk" without checking authenticity:</strong> look for the silk mark/GI and buy from trusted showrooms.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Underestimating time:</strong> a rushed single day can't cover both cities — plan 3–4 days.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not booking Tirumala darshan in advance:</strong> sort the ₹300 SED ticket for your Tirumala day before the trip.</li>
</ul>
