An NRI cousin landed in Tirupati straight from a 20-hour flight, dreading the famous 15-hour queue — and walked out after his darshan in barely 90 minutes. The secret was not a ₹10,000 donation or a politician's letter. It was the Supadham entrance — and most pilgrims have never even heard of it.

Supadham (often spelled Supatham) is not a "ticket" you buy — it is a designated entrance inside the Tirumala temple complex that serves as a fast, privileged route for specific categories of devotees. For NRIs and foreign nationals especially, it is the simplest legitimate way to a quick darshan, often free, with just a passport.

The confusion is that nobody explains clearly who can use Supadham, when, and with what documents. This guide fixes that — the eligibility, the NRI on-arrival fast entry, the timings, the exact location, and the documents you must carry.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many Tirumala trips, including helping NRI relatives navigate exactly this route. It genuinely works, but only if you reach the right gate, in the right window, with the right papers. Treat the details below as a 2026 snapshot and confirm the live rules on the official site, since TTD adjusts timings and categories.

<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — Supadham Entrance Darshan:
<ul>
  <li>📍 What it is: a privileged entrance/route inside the temple (near Vaikuntam I / the VQC) — not a buyable ticket.</li>
  <li>🌍 Who: NRIs & foreign nationals, Arjitha Seva devotees, and certain privileged/Special Entry holders.</li>
  <li>🛂 NRI entry: on-arrival with passport/OCI — generally free, no advance booking, priority darshan.</li>
  <li>🕛 Reporting: generally about 12:00 PM – 6:00 PM (verify the current window).</li>
  <li>⚡ Wait: short — typically under ~1–2 hours versus 8–20+ hours in the free line.</li>
  <li>🪪 Carry: passport (NRI/foreign), or your seva/darshan ticket if you qualify via that route.</li>
</ul></blockquote>

<h2>💡 Gap — Supadham is a route, not a ticket</h2>
This is the single biggest misunderstanding. People search "Supadham darshan ticket price" expecting to buy one — but Supadham is an <strong>entrance/gateway</strong> through which several categories of devotees enter for a faster darshan. You don't "buy Supadham"; you become eligible for it through one of these routes:
<ul>
  <li><strong>NRIs & foreign nationals</strong> — on-arrival, with a passport/OCI (the most common use)</li>
  <li><strong>Arjitha Seva devotees</strong> — those with a booked seva enter/exit via this route</li>
  <li><strong>Certain privileged / Special Entry Darshan holders</strong> — as designated by TTD</li>
</ul>
So the right question is not "how much is Supadham," but "which category lets me use the Supadham entrance?"

<h2>NRI & foreign-national fast entry via Supadham</h2>
This is the headline benefit. NRIs and foreign devotees can use Supadham as an on-arrival fast-darshan route:
<ul>
  <li><strong>Booking:</strong> generally no advance booking needed — report in person</li>
  <li><strong>Fee:</strong> for eligible NRIs/foreigners it is generally free (verify the current position)</li>
  <li><strong>Quota:</strong> there is no separate fixed "NRI quota" — your queue merges with others, but via the priority Supadham route</li>
  <li><strong>Wait:</strong> typically short — often under 1–2 hours</li>
</ul>

🛂 <strong>Documents to carry (NRI/foreign):</strong>
<ul>
  <li>Original <strong>passport</strong> (mandatory)</li>
  <li>OCI / PIO card if applicable</li>
  <li>For foreign nationals: passport with a valid visa</li>
  <li>Carry copies as backup</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> For NRIs, this on-arrival Supadham route is usually cheaper and just as quick as the premium options — so compare it with SRIVANI before spending ₹10,500. The fuller NRI picture (eligibility nuances, documents, what counts as NRI) is in the <a href="/nri-darshan-guide/">NRI darshan guide</a>.

<h2>Timings and reporting window</h2>
<ul>
  <li><strong>Reporting window:</strong> generally around 12:00 PM to 6:00 PM (some days extend a little; verify on the day)</li>
  <li><strong>Best time:</strong> reach early in the window (around 12–2 PM) to avoid the late-afternoon build-up</li>
  <li><strong>Daily:</strong> the privileged darshan via Supadham runs on most days, but can be restricted on heavy-rush/festival days</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Timings and category access via Supadham are set by TTD and change, and privileged routes can be curtailed during peak rush. Confirm the current window and eligibility on <a href="https://tirumala.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">tirumala.org</a> or at the Tirumala enquiry/JEO office on the day.

<h2>Where is the Supadham entrance?</h2>
<ul>
  <li>It is located inside the temple complex near the <strong>Vaikuntam I</strong> entrance, roughly 100–200 feet from the <a href="/vqc-vaikuntam-queue-complex-guide/">Vaikuntam Queue Complex (VQC)</a>.</li>
  <li>It is well within the main temple precinct — ask any TTD staff member for "Supadham/Supatham entrance" and they will point you there.</li>
  <li>NRIs typically first verify documents at the designated counter, then enter via this route.</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Don't waste time wandering — on reaching Tirumala, go straight to the enquiry desk and ask where NRIs/Supadham report today. The exact verification point can shift, and staff will direct you to the current one.

<h2>Arjitha Seva devotees and Supadham</h2>
If you have booked an <a href="/ttd-arjitha-sevas-list/">Arjitha Seva</a> (such as Kalyanotsavam or Abhishekam), your seva entry/exit is often routed through the Supadham area, since seva participants get close, privileged access. In that case the Supadham route is part of your seva experience — you don't need to arrange it separately.

<h2>Dress code and rules</h2>
The dress code applies at Supadham exactly as everywhere in the temple:
<ul>
  <li>✅ Men: dhoti/kurta or formal traditional wear</li>
  <li>✅ Women: saree or salwar-kameez</li>
  <li>❌ Jeans, shorts, sleeveless — refused</li>
  <li>📱 Phones into the locker before entry</li>
</ul>
See the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">Tirumala dress code rules</a>. The shorter Supadham wait does not exempt you from any of this.

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Searching for a "Supadham ticket to buy":</strong> it is a route, not a purchasable ticket. You qualify via the NRI/seva/privileged category — not by paying for "Supadham."</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>NRIs forgetting the passport:</strong> the passport (and OCI/visa if applicable) is mandatory for the NRI route — a photocopy alone or another ID won't do.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Reaching outside the reporting window:</strong> the window is roughly midday to evening — arrive too early or too late and you miss it. Confirm the day's timing first.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Assuming it runs on every festival day:</strong> privileged routes can be curtailed during heavy rush. Keep a ₹300/free backup on peak dates.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Wandering to find the gate:</strong> ask the enquiry desk on arrival — the verification point can change.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ignoring the dress code because the wait is short:</strong> the dress check applies here too — wrong dress means no entry.</li>
</ul>
