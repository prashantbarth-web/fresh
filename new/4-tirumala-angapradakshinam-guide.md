In the dark hours before dawn, while most pilgrims are still asleep, a line of devotees in wet clothes rolls their bodies along the cold stone streets around the Tirumala temple — eyes closed, chanting "Govinda Govinda." It is one of the most intense acts of devotion you will see anywhere, and it has a name: Angapradakshinam.

"Anga" means body and "Pradakshina" means circumambulation — so Angapradakshinam is circling the temple by rolling your body around it, rather than walking. Devotees do it to fulfil a deeply held vow (mokku) — a thanksgiving for a prayer answered, or a surrender of the ego. It is physical, humbling and, for those who do it, unforgettable.

The part most pilgrims get wrong in 2026 is the logistics: it is no longer a simple "turn up and roll." TTD now issues Angapradakshinam through an online token released in the monthly quota — and you must be staying on Tirumala the night before, because it happens in the pre-dawn hours.

I am Anant Venktashan — Delhi-based, with Andhra roots and many Tirumala visits. I have watched Angapradakshinam up close and helped family plan for it, and the difference between a smooth experience and a wasted trip is entirely in the preparation. Treat the details below as a 2026 snapshot and confirm the live rules on the official site, since TTD changes the token process.

<h2>What is Angapradakshinam?</h2>
Angapradakshinam is a vow-based ritual of rolling your body around the temple, along the streets (mada veedhulu) surrounding the main shrine.
<ul>
  <li><strong>Meaning:</strong> anga (body) + pradakshina (clockwise circumambulation) — circling by rolling, not walking</li>
  <li><strong>Why devotees do it:</strong> to fulfil a vow — gratitude for a prayer answered, a wish granted, or as supreme surrender</li>
  <li><strong>How:</strong> after a holy bath, in wet clothes, the devotee lies down and rolls their body around the temple's outer streets</li>
  <li><strong>When:</strong> in the pre-dawn hours, before the day's regular darshan</li>
  <li><strong>Spirit:</strong> it represents complete humility — putting the body itself at the Lord's service</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Like the <a href="/tirumala-mokkuvalu-head-tonsure/">head tonsure (mokkuvalu)</a>, Angapradakshinam is a personal vow — not compulsory and not "better" than any other form of worship. People undertake it for their own reasons, most often a manauti fulfilled. There is no judgement either way.

<h2>💡 Gap — the 2026 change: it now needs an online token</h2>
Here is what trips up people using old information. Angapradakshinam is no longer a free walk-up ritual you can join on the spot. Because the numbers must be managed, <strong>TTD now releases Angapradakshinam tokens online</strong> as part of the monthly quota.

Roughly how it works (confirm the exact dates each month on tirumala.org):
<ul>
  <li>TTD announces a monthly online quota schedule, typically a couple of months ahead.</li>
  <li>Angapradakshinam tokens are released online at a set time (commonly around 10 AM on the announced date).</li>
  <li>The token is free, but the quota is limited and can go quickly.</li>
  <li>You carry the token confirmation and report at the designated point in the early morning.</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Without a valid token you generally cannot join Angapradakshinam on busy days. Book it when the month's quota opens — see <a href="/ttd-darshan-dates-kaise-check-karen/">how to check TTD release dates</a>. On heavy-rush/festival days the activity can be restricted, so confirm on <a href="https://tirumala.org" target="_blank" rel="noopener">tirumala.org</a>.

<h2>💡 Gap — the process, step by step</h2>

<h3>The night before</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Stay on Tirumala — <a href="/tirumala-stay-options-pilgrims/">book accommodation</a> in advance (the pre-dawn timing makes a hill stay essential)</li>
  <li>Light dinner, early sleep — you'll wake around 2–3 AM</li>
  <li>Keep the token confirmation + original ID ready</li>
  <li>Keep a set of clothes you don't mind getting wet and dirty</li>
</ul>

<h3>Pre-dawn morning</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Wake very early and take a holy bath (the Pushkarini tank or a bath as directed)</li>
  <li>Wear wet clothes — men typically a dhoti; women a saree (modest, secure)</li>
  <li>Report at the designated point with your token at the instructed time</li>
  <li>Verification — token + ID checked</li>
</ul>

<h3>The roll itself</h3>
<ul>
  <li>You begin from the designated start and roll your body clockwise along the temple's outer streets</li>
  <li>It is done in a continuous, disciplined line with fellow devotees, chanting "Govinda Govinda"</li>
  <li>Staff and volunteers manage the route and pace</li>
  <li>Distance/time varies, but expect a physically demanding stretch on stone surfaces</li>
</ul>

<h3>After</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Change into clean, dry traditional clothes</li>
  <li>Proceed for darshan via the designated route</li>
  <li>Collect <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">laddu prasadam</a> afterwards</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> The streets are cold and hard in the pre-dawn hours. Devotees who do it describe the discomfort dissolving into a kind of meditative surrender. Mentally prepare for the physical part — it is meant to be humbling, not comfortable.

<h2>What to wear and carry</h2>
<ul>
  <li>✅ Men: dhoti (the roll is done in wet dhoti); a simple upper cloth</li>
  <li>✅ Women: saree, securely worn (modesty and safety)</li>
  <li>✅ A dry change of traditional clothes for darshan afterwards</li>
  <li>✅ A towel</li>
  <li>✅ Token confirmation (print) + original ID</li>
  <li>✅ A small amount of antiseptic/balm — minor scrapes are possible on stone</li>
  <li>❌ No phones, no valuables during the roll — leave them safely</li>
</ul>

<h2>💡 Gap — who should and shouldn't do it</h2>
<ul>
  <li>✅ <strong>Suited to:</strong> reasonably fit adults fulfilling a genuine vow, mentally prepared for the physical demand</li>
  <li>⚠️ <strong>Think carefully:</strong> those unused to physical exertion — the rolling on hard ground is tougher than it looks</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not advised:</strong> senior citizens with health issues, pregnant women, people with heart/spine/blood-pressure conditions, or anyone advised against strenuous activity</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Young children:</strong> not appropriate</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> This is genuinely strenuous — rolling on cold stone in the early morning. If you have any health condition, consult a doctor first, and consider that devotion has many forms. A heartfelt <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">regular darshan</a> or a seva is equally valid; never risk your health.

<h2>Dress code and conduct</h2>
The temple <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">dress code</a> applies for the darshan that follows. During the roll, traditional wet clothing is the norm. Maintain silence and discipline, follow staff instructions, and respect fellow devotees' space and pace throughout.

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Expecting to join on the spot:</strong> Angapradakshinam now needs an online token from the monthly quota. Book it when the quota opens.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Planning it while staying in Tirupati town:</strong> the pre-dawn timing makes that impossible — you must stay on Tirumala the night before.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Underestimating the physical demand:</strong> rolling on hard stone is strenuous. Prepare your body and mind; don't attempt it with a health condition.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not carrying dry clothes:</strong> you roll in wet clothes, then need clean dry traditional wear for darshan. Pack a change + towel.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ignoring health limits:</strong> seniors with ailments, pregnant women and heart/spine patients should not attempt it — choose a regular darshan or seva instead.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Booking through an agent:</strong> the token is free and official — only via the TTD portal. Never pay a broker for "Angapradakshinam booking."</li>
</ul>
