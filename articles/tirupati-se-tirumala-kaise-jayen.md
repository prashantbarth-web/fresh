You step off the train at Tirupati, look up at the hills, and think, "The temple's right there — but how do I actually get up?" An auto driver quotes ₹800, someone says "take the bus," someone else says "trek." First-timers often don't realise that Tirupati city and the Tirumala temple are two different places, about 22 km apart, with the temple sitting up a winding ghat road.

That gap is exactly where people lose time and money. Pick the wrong option and you either overpay a tout or end up stuck in a long bus queue at the worst hour. Pick the right one and the climb to the Lord's hill is smooth, cheap, and even beautiful.

This guide covers every way to get from Tirupati to Tirumala in 2026 — the bus (free TTD and paid APSRTC), the two footpaths, cabs, and the helicopter — with honest fares, the real distances, and which option suits whom. It's written for first-timers and families who want a clear answer.

A note from experience: I've made this climb several times — by bus, on the Alipiri footpath, and by cab with family. Each has its place, and this guide lays them out honestly so you can choose what fits your group.

<ul>
  <li>🚌 <strong>Bus (best):</strong> Free TTD buses and paid APSRTC buses (~₹50), every few minutes, ~40–70 min up the ~22 km ghat road.</li>
  <li>🚖 <strong>Cab:</strong> ₹800–1,200 one-way (drops you at Alipiri base — no private vehicles up top).</li>
  <li>🚶 <strong>Alipiri footpath:</strong> Free, ~7.8 km, ~3 hours — gives Divya Darshan priority.</li>
  <li>🚶 <strong>Srivari Mettu:</strong> Free, ~2 km, ~1.5 hours — shorter, steeper.</li>
  <li>🚁 <strong>Helicopter:</strong> Premium, a few minutes — book ahead if available (best for mobility issues).</li>
  <li>🪪 Carry original Aadhaar; return bus tickets bought in Tirupati are valid for 3 days.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Tirupati vs Tirumala — clear this first</h2>
This trips up almost every first-timer. <strong>Tirupati</strong> is the city in the plains — railway station, airport, hotels, bus stands. <strong>Tirumala</strong> is the hilltop town about 22 km above it, where the Venkateswara Swami temple sits at around 850 m. Between them runs the ghat road. So whatever route you choose, you're going <em>up</em> from Tirupati to Tirumala — there's no temple in Tirupati city itself. How the darshan works once you're up there is in <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">types of Tirumala darshan</a>.
<h2>💡 Gap: free TTD bus vs paid APSRTC bus (and the 3-day ticket)</h2>
Most guides just say "take the bus" without explaining that there are two kinds, which is the most useful thing to know:
<ul>
  <li><strong>APSRTC buses</strong> run continuously between Tirupati and Tirumala up the ghat road for a small fare (around ₹50), every few minutes, roughly 40–70 minutes each way.</li>
  <li><strong>Free TTD buses</strong> run from the Tirupati railway station to Alipiri for pilgrims who intend to <em>walk up</em> the footpath — handy if you're trekking.</li>
</ul>
A genuinely useful tip: buy your <strong>return ticket in Tirupati itself</strong> — it's valid for three days, so you can board any returning bus within that window without queuing for a ticket on the hill. Board near the railway station, the RTC/Central bus stand, or Alipiri. There are security checks on the ghat road, so carry original ID.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Fares and timings are revised periodically — confirm current rates on <a href="https://www.tirumala.org/" rel="nofollow">tirumala.org</a>. In peak season (Brahmotsavam, Vaikunta Ekadasi, holidays), the bus queue can run 1–2 hours; the early-morning (3–5 AM) buses are the lightest.
<h2>Option 1 — Bus: the practical choice</h2>
For almost everyone, the bus is the way up: cheap, frequent, safe, and it drops you at the Tirumala bus stand within walking distance of the Vaikuntam Queue Complex (no extra auto needed). You pay the conductor on board — keep small change and carry original Aadhaar. It's ideal for first-timers, solo travellers and budget pilgrims. For a family of four, the bus is a fraction of a cab fare, though a cab buys door-to-door comfort.
<h2>Option 2 — Cab / taxi</h2>
With elders, small children or heavy luggage, a cab is comfortable: a prepaid taxi from the railway station counter or an app/local cab runs about ₹800–1,200 one-way, or ₹1,800–2,500 for a round trip where the driver waits.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> A cab can only take you to the Alipiri base — personal vehicles are restricted on Tirumala. Park at Alipiri and take a TTD/APSRTC bus up. The driver knows this and will drop you at the right point. Use the station's prepaid counter (fixed fare) rather than agreeing to a tout's quote outside.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> The Alipiri path is fully illuminated, so many pilgrims start around 2–3 AM to reach the top by sunrise. Wear non-leather shoes (leather isn't allowed in the temple), carry water and light snacks, and keep a small backpack. Deep dives are in the <a href="/alipiri-footpath-trek-guide/">Alipiri footpath guide</a> and the <a href="/srivari-mettu-footpath-guide/">Srivari Mettu guide</a>; the priority line is explained in the <a href="/divya-darshan-free-token-guide/">Divya Darshan guide</a>.

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Collect the footpath token at the base (commonly the Bhudevi Complex for Alipiri) — it's Aadhaar-based and needed for Divya Darshan. Descending strains the knees, so for the return many trekkers take the bus down.
<h2>Option 5 — Helicopter</h2>
A premium helicopter service has operated between the Tirupati side and Tirumala, cutting the journey to a few minutes — useful for senior citizens, the differently-abled, or those very short on time. Seats are limited and it needs advance booking, and the service can be intermittent, so confirm current availability and price on tirumala.org. Note it only gets you up the hill — it doesn't skip the darshan queue.
<h2>No private vehicles up top — the parking rule</h2>
General pilgrims can't drive personal cars or bikes onto Tirumala. Park at the TTD lot at Alipiri base and continue by bus or on foot. Only special cases (medical emergencies, certain TTD vehicles) get permission. So plan to switch to a bus at Alipiri regardless of how you reach the base.

<h2>Before you go up — sort darshan & packing</h2>
Getting up the hill matters less than having your darshan sorted. Book the <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">₹300 Special Entry Darshan</a> in advance if you can; otherwise free Sarva Darshan needs an SSD token and a long wait (see the <a href="/tirumala-darshan-waiting-time/">waiting time guide</a>), and trekkers get Divya Darshan priority. Pack non-leather shoes, water and traditional clothes using the <a href="/tirupati-packing-list/">packing list</a>, and read the <a href="/vqc-vaikuntam-queue-complex-guide/">VQC guide</a> for what happens once you reach the queue complex.
<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — Tirupati to Tirumala</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Looking for the temple in Tirupati city:</strong> It's 22 km up the hill — plan the climb separately.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Saying yes to the first tout's quote:</strong> Use the station prepaid counter or just take the bus.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Trying to drive up:</strong> Personal vehicles stop at Alipiri — park and take the bus.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Trekking in leather shoes:</strong> Leather isn't allowed in the temple — wear non-leather footwear.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Going up at 6–9 AM:</strong> The busiest window — prefer early morning or evening.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not planning the return:</strong> Buy the 3-day return ticket in Tirupati and account for the evening queue.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Queuing for darshan straight after a trek:</strong> Rest and rehydrate first.</li>
</ul>
