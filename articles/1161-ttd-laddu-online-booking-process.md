A message lands: "Coming from Tirupati? Bring laddus." But the counter on Tirumala gives only a couple per person, and the family list back home has ten names. Or you did not travel at all, and there is a big occasion at home that calls for TTD's authentic prasadam. In both cases the answer is the same — TTD's official online laddu order.

The only hurdle is that the portal feels confusing the first time. This article is built purely for that: the exact booking process, step by step, screen by screen, with the payment and tracking parts spelled out so nothing trips you up.

No history, no price debates — just a clean walkthrough from opening the site to receiving the parcel, plus a troubleshooting section for when payment goes through but the confirmation does not.

I have ordered the proforma laddu online myself, and once you have done it once, it takes five minutes the next time. So treat the rates and timelines below as 2026 estimates, then confirm live details on the official portal before paying.

<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — TTD laddu online booking:
Open tirumala.org → "Proforma Laddu Booking" → Login/Register → fill quantity and delivery address → pay about ₹200 per laddu → confirmation comes by SMS/email. Delivery in 3–7 working days. Book only on tirumala.org — no third-party site is authentic.</blockquote>

<h2>What you need before you start</h2>
Keep these ready before opening the portal:
<ul>
  <li>✅ Mobile number — for OTP verification</li>
  <li>✅ Email ID — for the confirmation</li>
  <li>✅ Delivery address — pincode, flat/house number, landmark</li>
  <li>✅ Payment method — UPI / debit-credit card / net banking</li>
  <li>✅ Aadhaar number — sometimes asked at registration</li>
</ul>
That is all — nothing more is required.

<h2>Step-by-step booking process</h2>

<h3>Step 1 — Open the correct website</h3>
Type into your browser: <strong>tirumala.org</strong>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Searching "TTD laddu online order" on Google throws up many third-party sites claiming to sell "authentic Tirupati laddu." These are not genuine. Only <strong>tirumala.org</strong> is the official TTD portal.

<h3>Step 2 — Find "Proforma Laddu Booking"</h3>
On the homepage:
<ul>
  <li>Click the "Online Services" menu</li>
  <li>In the dropdown, find "Proforma Laddu Booking"</li>
  <li>Click it</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> TTD updates the portal layout from time to time. If you cannot find the exact menu, type "Laddu" in the homepage search box and the direct link appears.

<h3>Step 3 — Login or register</h3>
<strong>First time — Register:</strong>
<ul>
  <li>Enter your mobile number</li>
  <li>Verify the OTP</li>
  <li>Fill in name and email</li>
  <li>Set a password</li>
</ul>
<strong>Already have an account — Login:</strong>
<ul>
  <li>Enter your registered mobile number and password</li>
  <li>Or use the OTP login option</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> If you have previously booked a darshan ticket or cottage on the TTD portal, the same account works — no need to create a new one.

<h3>Step 4 — Select quantity</h3>
After login, the Proforma Laddu booking page opens:
<ul>
  <li>Available quantity options are shown</li>
  <li>Select your quantity — the maximum limit is displayed on the portal and varies with demand</li>
  <li>Rate: about <strong>₹200 per laddu</strong> (delivery charges included)</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Before festivals (Navratri, Diwali, Brahmotsavam) TTD reduces the per-order limit so more pilgrims get equal access. The current limit shows automatically at checkout.

<h3>Step 5 — Fill in the delivery address</h3>
<ul>
  <li>Receiver's full name</li>
  <li>Complete address — flat/house number, street, area</li>
  <li>City and State</li>
  <li>PIN code — enter it accurately; this determines the delivery route</li>
  <li>Mobile number — the courier will call on this</li>
  <li>Landmark — always add one</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> A wrong address means failed delivery. Verify the PIN code once — you can cross-check it on Google Maps.

<h3>Step 6 — Make the payment</h3>
Payment options:
<ul>
  <li><strong>UPI</strong> — fastest and recommended; GPay, PhonePe and Paytm all work</li>
  <li><strong>Debit / Credit card</strong> — Visa, Mastercard, RuPay</li>
  <li><strong>Net banking</strong> — all major banks</li>
</ul>
After payment:
<ul>
  <li>An order confirmation screen appears — take a screenshot</li>
  <li>A confirmation letter arrives by email</li>
  <li>An SMS carries the Order ID and tracking details</li>
</ul>

<h3>Step 7 — Track the order</h3>
<ul>
  <li>The confirmation email contains a courier tracking link</li>
  <li>You can also track at tirumala.org → "Order Status"</li>
  <li>The courier partner calls before delivery</li>
</ul>

<h2>💡 Gap — payment went through but no confirmation: what to do</h2>
This is the most common worry, so here is the clear sequence:
<ol>
  <li><strong>Do not pay again.</strong> A second payment risks a double order.</li>
  <li><strong>Wait 24–48 hours.</strong> The confirmation email usually arrives automatically.</li>
  <li><strong>Check your bank/UPI.</strong> If money was debited, the order is almost certainly registered.</li>
  <li><strong>Keep the payment reference.</strong> The UPI/transaction reference number is your proof.</li>
  <li><strong>Still nothing after 48 hours?</strong> Use the official support/grievance channel listed on tirumala.org with your payment reference.</li>
</ol>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Raise any issue only through the contact and grievance channels on tirumala.org. Avoid phone numbers or emails forwarded on WhatsApp or social media — many are fake and used for fraud.

<h2>💡 Gap — what to check the moment it arrives</h2>
When you receive the parcel, verify:
<ul>
  <li>✅ Packaging is sealed and intact</li>
  <li>✅ The official TTD label is present</li>
  <li>✅ A manufacture/best-before date is on the label</li>
  <li>✅ The laddu has the authentic ghee-and-cardamom aroma</li>
</ul>
❌ If the packaging is damaged, the seal is broken, or the laddu smells off, report it within 24 hours (with a photo) through the official support channel on tirumala.org.

<h2>Shelf life — how long will it stay fresh?</h2>
<ul>
  <li>Room temperature: 7–10 days from the delivery date</li>
  <li>Refrigerator: up to about 15 days</li>
  <li>Freezing: not recommended</li>
</ul>

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> In North Indian summers (April–June) room temperatures cross 40°C — refrigerate the laddu as soon as it arrives.

<h2>❌ Common mistakes</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ordering from a third-party site:</strong> the first Google result is not TTD. Always tirumala.org — no shortcut.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ordering two days before a festival:</strong> processing + delivery is 6–10 days minimum. Order three weeks ahead.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Skipping the landmark:</strong> despite the PIN code, couriers rely on landmarks — always fill one in.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Closing the browser after payment:</strong> shutting it before the confirmation screen can leave the order stuck. Wait for the confirmation.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not saving the Order ID:</strong> you need it for both tracking and any complaint — screenshot the email confirmation.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Leaving it at room temperature in summer:</strong> for more than 3–4 days in the heat it turns sticky — refrigerate.</li>
</ul>
