<h2>What is an Arjitha Seva?</h2>
The Tirumala temple has two kinds of sevas.

<strong>Nitya Sevas</strong> are the daily rituals TTD performs as part of temple worship — pilgrims do not participate in these directly.

<strong>Arjitha Sevas</strong> are the ones pilgrims book for themselves. "Arjitha" means earned or acquired — a seva you take part in with your offering and devotion. During an Arjitha Seva you get close access inside the temple, sometimes near the inner sanctum, and that closeness is the real value.

💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> From my own visits, a seva like Kalyanotsavam feels fundamentally different from a regular darshan — a shorter queue, a defined slot, and a few extra moments before Lord Venkateswara that genuinely stay with you.

<h2>💡 Gap — the biggest 2026 change: the Electronic Dip</h2>
This is what trips up almost everyone using old information. Several of the most sought-after Arjitha Sevas are no longer booked on a simple "30 days in advance, first-come" basis. Demand far outstrips slots, so TTD moved them to an <strong>Electronic Dip</strong> — a computerised lucky-dip lottery.

Here is roughly how it works (confirm the exact dates each month on the official site):
<ul>
  <li>TTD announces a monthly online quota schedule — usually a couple of months ahead.</li>
  <li>The dip for sevas such as Suprabhatam, Thomala, Archana and Ashtadala Pada Padmaradhana opens at a set time (commonly 10 AM on the announced date).</li>
  <li>You register your entry in the dip within the registration window (typically a day or two).</li>
  <li>If you are selected, you pay within a short payment window (often a 2–3 day window, before a noon cut-off) to confirm the ticket.</li>
  <li>No selection means no charge — you simply try again next month.</li>
</ul>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> Because it is a lottery, no amount of "logging in at midnight" guarantees a slot. Register cleanly within the window, keep your details correct, and have a backup plan. The detailed walkthrough is in the <a href="/ttd-seva-ticket-booking/">TTD seva ticket booking guide</a>.

<h2>Seva vs Darshan — clear this confusion first</h2>
Many pilgrims assume that booking a seva automatically gives them darshan. It does not.

A seva ticket and a darshan ticket are completely separate. During a seva you enter the temple for that specific ritual and get close access during it — but it does not replace a regular or <a href="/ttd-special-entry-darshan-online-booking/">₹300 Special Entry Darshan</a> ticket for a general visit.

✅ The ideal plan: book your seva and your darshan ticket separately. To understand how all the darshan options fit together, read <a href="/tirumala-darshan-ke-prakar/">types of Tirumala darshan</a>. And to weigh up which sevas are worth paying for, see the <a href="/free-vs-paid-sevas-tirumala/">free vs paid sevas comparison</a>.

<h2>How to book — step by step</h2>
<ol>
  <li>Go to tirumala.org / the official TTD portal and open the Arjitha Seva / Electronic Dip section.</li>
  <li>Check the month's announced quota schedule and the dip date for your chosen seva.</li>
  <li>Register your entry in the dip within the registration window, entering each pilgrim's correct details and Aadhaar.</li>
  <li>Keep your gothram and nakshatra ready — some sevas require them.</li>
  <li>If selected, complete payment within the payment window (UPI is fastest), then download and save the confirmation PDF.</li>
</ol>

⚠️ <strong>Important:</strong> After payment, both download and screenshot the confirmation. At check-in you must show this document with the matching original ID for each named person.

<h2>Which seva should you book first?</h2>
For first-timers, a simple recommendation:
<ul>
  <li>✅ <strong>Couples:</strong> Kalyanotsavam — the most meaningful shared experience (around ₹1,000 per couple).</li>
  <li>✅ <strong>Solo or family, visual impact:</strong> Sahasra Deepalankara — the thousand-lamp evening ceremony is unforgettable.</li>
  <li>✅ <strong>Early risers:</strong> Suprabhatam or Thomala — the pre-dawn atmosphere is unique (via the Electronic Dip).</li>
  <li>✅ <strong>Closest access:</strong> Abhishekam — among the most intimate sevas, and highly sought.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Skip on a first trip:</strong> the premium festival/Brahmotsavam sevas — complex, expensive and limited.</li>
</ul>

<h2>❌ Common mistakes pilgrims make</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Treating a seva ticket as a darshan ticket:</strong> they are separate — book both.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Expecting simple first-come booking:</strong> popular sevas now go through the Electronic Dip lottery; plan around the dip dates.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not knowing your gothram and nakshatra:</strong> several sevas need them — confirm with family elders beforehand, as there is no time to figure it out inside.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Reaching late:</strong> sevas are strictly time-bound; arrive at the reporting point well before the slot, or you lose entry.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Ignoring the dress code:</strong> traditional attire is mandatory for sevas — dhoti for men, saree or salwar-kameez for women. See the <a href="/tirumala-dress-code-rules/">Tirumala dress code rules</a>.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Carrying a phone in:</strong> phones are not allowed inside — deposit them in the free lockers.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Relying on an old price list:</strong> seva rates change; verify the current cost on tirumala.org.</li>
</ul>
