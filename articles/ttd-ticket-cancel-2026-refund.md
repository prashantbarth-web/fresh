Your ₹300 darshan ticket is booked, the train is reserved, the hotel is locked in — and then life happens. Someone at home falls ill, work suddenly explodes, or a family emergency lands. The very first question in your head is: "Can I cancel the TTD ticket? Will I get my money back?"

Let's be honest and clear: TTD's cancellation policy is strict, but it is not the same for everything. A ₹300 darshan ticket follows one rule, a seva ticket another, accommodation another, and a failed payment something else entirely. People lose money — or wrongly expect refunds — simply because they mix these up.

This guide spells out the real 2026 rules for each case: what comes back, what doesn't, how failed-payment refunds work, and a couple of things hardly anyone knows (like still collecting laddu if your darshan didn't happen). It's written so you know exactly where you stand before you pay.

A note from experience: I once had to abandon a trip at short notice and called the helpline hoping for a darshan refund. The reply was simple — the ticket is non-refundable. It stings, but it's how TTD keeps black-marketing in check while managing tens of thousands of pilgrims a day.

<blockquote>⚡ Quick Answer — TTD cancellation & refund:
<ul>
  <li>❌ ₹300 Special Entry Darshan — non-cancellable, non-refundable, non-transferable.</li>
  <li>❌ Arjitha Seva tickets — same: no cancellation, no refund.</li>
  <li>✅ TTD accommodation — cancel online, refund (generally ~7 working days).</li>
  <li>✅ Failed/stuck payment — auto-refunded; no complaint needed.</li>
  <li>❌ No-show — ticket forfeited, zero refund.</li>
  <li>🍬 Missed darshan? You can still claim laddu prasadam with your ticket + ID.</li>
</ul>
</blockquote>
<h2>₹300 SED ticket — is it refundable?</h2>
Straight answer: <strong>no.</strong> Under TTD policy, once a Special Entry Darshan (₹300) ticket is booked it cannot be cancelled, postponed or transferred. This is written into the Terms & Conditions you accept at booking — which most people tick past without reading.

<h2>Arjitha Seva tickets — the same strict rule</h2>
Kalyanotsavam, Abhishekam, Sahasra Deepalankara and other Arjitha Sevas carry the identical policy — no cancellation, no refund, no postponement, no transfer. Sevas are booked far in advance and demand is enormous, so lock your dates first, then book the seva. The booking flow is in the <a href="/ttd-seva-ticket-booking/">TTD seva booking guide</a>.
<h2>TTD accommodation — this one IS refundable ✅</h2>
Here's the relief: TTD accommodation supports cancellation and refund. If you've booked a TTD room or guest house, cancel it online:
<ol>
  <li>Log in to the official TTD portal (ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in) with the account used for booking.</li>
  <li>Open <strong>My Bookings</strong> → <strong>Accommodation</strong>.</li>
  <li>Select the booking, choose <strong>Cancel</strong>, and confirm.</li>
  <li>The refund returns to your original payment method, generally within about 7 working days.</li>
</ol>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Cancel as early as possible — a no-show forfeits the accommodation refund too. Save the cancellation confirmation. TTD refunds only electronically (to your UPI/card/net-banking source), never in cash. Booking details are in the <a href="/ttd-accommodation-online-booking/">accommodation booking guide</a>.
<h2>💡 Gap: a failed payment auto-refunds — don't panic</h2>
This is the most common scare, and most guides don't calm it down. Money is deducted but the ticket shows "Transaction Failed" or "Pending." Don't panic — that amount is <strong>refunded automatically</strong>. No complaint, no email required.

<h2>When TTD cancels darshan from its side</h2>
Sometimes TTD itself suspends darshan for a day — a natural calamity, VIP visit, technical issue or temple maintenance. In those cases TTD usually offers an alternative date or a refund, but it may not happen automatically — you may need to reach out. Use only the official contact channels listed on tirumala.org, quoting your booking ID, name, date, ticket type and a clear description. Ignore random email addresses or "agents" claiming to process TTD refunds — that's where scams creep in.
<h2>💡 Gap: no darshan? You can still claim laddu prasadam</h2>
Very few pilgrims know this, and it genuinely softens a missed trip. If your darshan didn't happen but you reached Tirupati, you can still go to the <strong>laddu prasadam counter and claim laddu</strong> — even without completing darshan.

What you need:
<ul>
  <li>✅ Original ticket printout (or mobile screenshot)</li>
  <li>✅ Valid photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN or Passport)</li>
</ul>
Generally one laddu per person. The laddu counter has its own queue, so go early — in peak season it can take 1–2 hours. More in the <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">laddu prasadam guide</a>.
<h2>How to track your refund</h2>
You can check refund status online — log in to the official TTD portal, open the Refund / Transaction History section, and enter your Transaction ID to see the current status. If it reads "Processed" but the money still hasn't reached your account after the timelines above, take it up with your bank, since TTD's responsibility ends once the transfer is initiated. The <a href="/ttd-mobile-app-guide/">TTD app</a> also shows your bookings and history.
<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — cancellation & refunds</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Booking before confirming travel:</strong> Plans shift and the ₹300 ticket is non-refundable — lock dates first.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Mistyping the name:</strong> It's matched to ID at the gate, can't be edited, and a mismatch blocks entry.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Skipping the Terms & Conditions:</strong> "Non-cancellable, non-refundable" is stated there — no surprises later.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Expecting a refund after a no-show:</strong> Unused tickets are forfeited completely.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not cancelling accommodation before skipping it:</strong> It's refundable only if cancelled; a no-show forfeits it.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Panic-complaining after a failed payment:</strong> It auto-reverses — wait the window before involving the bank.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Passing the ticket to someone else:</strong> Tickets are non-transferable; both people get refused at the gate.</li>
</ul>
