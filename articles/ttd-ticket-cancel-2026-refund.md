The ₹300 SED ticket is booked, the train is reserved, the hotel is confirmed — and then someone at home falls ill, or work blows up, or a family emergency hits. The first question that races through your mind is: "Will the TTD ticket cancel? Will I get my money back?"

Let me be straight: TTD's cancellation policy is strict, but not every situation is the same. The ₹300 darshan ticket has one rule, accommodation another, and a failed payment a completely different one. Mixing these up is why people either lose money they could've recovered or expect refunds that were never possible.

This guide lays out the truth for each case in 2026 — what's refundable, what isn't, how failed-payment refunds work, and a couple of lesser-known things (like claiming laddu even if darshan didn't happen). It's written so you know exactly where you stand before you book.

A note from experience: I once had to drop a trip on short notice and called the TTD helpline hoping for a darshan refund. The answer was clear — the ticket is non-refundable. It felt harsh, but it's how TTD keeps black-marketing in check while handling tens of thousands of pilgrims daily.

  <li>✅ Failed/stuck payment — auto-refunded, no complaint needed.</li>
  <li>❌ No-show — ticket forfeited, zero refund.</li>

<h2>₹300 SED ticket — is it refundable?</h2>
The direct answer: <strong>no.</strong> Per TTD policy, once a Special Entry Darshan (₹300) ticket is booked, it cannot be cancelled, postponed or transferred. This is stated in the Terms & Conditions you accept at booking — which most people tick through without reading.

<h2>Arjitha Seva tickets — same strict rule</h2>
Kalyanotsavam, Abhishekam, Sahasra Deepalankara and other Arjitha Sevas follow the same policy: no cancellation, no refund, no postponement, no transfer. Sevas are booked far in advance and in huge demand, so lock your travel dates first, then book the seva. The booking process is in the <a href="/ttd-seva-ticket-booking/">TTD seva booking guide</a>.
<h2>TTD accommodation — this one IS refundable ✅</h2>
The good news: TTD accommodation supports cancellation and refund. If you've booked a TTD room or guest house, you can cancel it online:
<ol>
  <li>Log in to the official TTD portal (ttdevasthanams.ap.gov.in) with your booking account.</li>
  <li>Go to <strong>My Bookings</strong> → <strong>Accommodation</strong>.</li>
  <li>Select the booking and choose <strong>Cancel</strong>, then confirm.</li>
  <li>The refund goes back to your original payment method, generally within about 7 working days.</li>
</ol>
💡 <strong>Insider Tip:</strong> Cancel as early as you can — a no-show forfeits the accommodation refund too. Save the cancellation confirmation. TTD refunds only electronically (to your UPI/card/net-banking source), never in cash. Booking details are in the <a href="/ttd-accommodation-online-booking/">accommodation booking guide</a>.
<h2>💡 Gap: failed payment? It auto-refunds — don't panic</h2>
This is the most common scare, and most guides don't explain it calmly. The amount is deducted but the ticket shows "Transaction Failed" or "Pending." Don't panic — that amount is <strong>automatically refunded</strong>. You don't need to file a complaint or send an email.

<h2>When TTD cancels darshan from its side</h2>
Occasionally TTD itself suspends darshan for a day — a natural calamity, VIP visit, technical issue or maintenance. In such cases TTD usually offers an alternative date or a refund, but it may not be automatic — you may need to contact them. Use only the official contact channels listed on tirumala.org, with your booking ID, name, date, ticket type and a clear description. Don't trust random email addresses or "agents" claiming to process TTD refunds.
<h2>💡 Gap: no darshan? You can still claim laddu prasadam</h2>
Here's something very few pilgrims know, and it genuinely helps. If your darshan didn't happen for some reason but you reached Tirupati, you can still go to the <strong>laddu prasadam counter and claim laddu</strong> — without darshan.

What you need:
<ul>
  <li>✅ Original ticket printout (or mobile screenshot)</li>
  <li>✅ Valid photo ID (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport)</li>
</ul>
Generally one laddu per person. There's a queue at the laddu counter too, so go early. More on prasadam in the <a href="/tirumala-laddu-prasad-guide/">laddu prasadam guide</a>.
<h2>How to track your refund status</h2>
You can check refund status online: log in to the official TTD portal, open the Refund/Transaction History section, and enter your Transaction ID to see the current status. If it shows "Processed" but the money hasn't reached your account after the timeline above, contact your bank — TTD's responsibility ends once the transfer is initiated. The <a href="/ttd-mobile-app-guide/">TTD app</a> also shows your bookings and history.
<h2>❌ Common Mistakes — cancellation & refunds</h2>
<ul>
  <li>❌ <strong>Booking before confirming travel:</strong> Plans change, and the ₹300 ticket is non-refundable. Lock dates first.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Typing the wrong name:</strong> It's matched to ID at the gate, can't be changed, and a mismatch blocks entry.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Skipping the Terms & Conditions:</strong> "Non-cancellable, non-refundable" is stated there — no surprises later.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Expecting a refund after a no-show:</strong> Unused tickets are forfeited entirely.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Not cancelling accommodation before skipping it:</strong> Accommodation is refundable only if you cancel — a no-show forfeits it.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Panic-complaining after a failed payment:</strong> It auto-reverses; wait the window before involving the bank.</li>
  <li>❌ <strong>Trying to pass the ticket to someone else:</strong> Tickets are non-transferable — both people get refused at the gate.</li>
</ul>
