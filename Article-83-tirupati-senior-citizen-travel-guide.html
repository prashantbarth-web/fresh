  • Best entry: ₹300 Special Entry — AC, seated, shortest queue
  • Transport: Direct bus (seated) — avoid walking path

  • Stay: Ground floor TTD rooms — request specifically

⚠️ जरूरी बात: Regular free queue (4-8 hours, mostly standing) — senior citizens के लिए recommend नहीं। Dehydration, knee pain, fatigue, BP spike — medical risks real। ₹300 paid entry या 65+ priority = best options। ₹300 per person spend करें — it's for health and safety, not luxury। 💡 Insider Tip: TTD wheelchair + companion = complete darshan without standing। Temple entry → sanctum → exit = wheelchair route exists (ramp access)। Request counter पर — "wheelchair for elderly darshan" बोलें। One family member pushes wheelchair — TTD staff guide route। Sanctum entry: briefly stand/be supported for 10-15 seconds darshan — then back to wheelchair।

क्या गलतियाँ करते हैं लोग (और कैसे avoid करें)

  • ❌ Walking path (Alipiri) attempt करवाना elderly को: 3,500 steps = 3-4 hours trek। Young fit people के लिए challenging — elderly impossible/dangerous। Bus/taxi only। No exceptions for knee/heart patients।
    • 👉 Related: Alipiri footpath trek guide — इसे भी ज़रूर पढ़ें।
  • ❌ Peak crowd days choose करना: Weekends, festivals = extreme crowd, pushing, long standing। Senior citizens fragile — crowd injuries possible। Weekday (Tue-Thu) = 50% less crowd, safer, comfortable।
  • ❌ Medicines hotel room में छोड़ देना: Temple complex 3-5 hours minimum। Medicines (BP, diabetes, heart) = ALWAYS carry on person। Small pouch — neck/waist। Emergency can happen anywhere — immediate access ज़रूरी।
  • ❌ Wheelchair पहले से request न करना: Temple entry पर directly जाकर माँगना = available (usually), but sometimes wait 15-20 min। Better: information counter पर 30 min पहले book/request। Guaranteed availability।
  • ❌ अकेले elderly को queue में छोड़ना: "Main laddu leke aata hun" — absolutely not। Crowd unpredictable — elderly alone = fall risk, disorientation, panic। Always one companion WITH elderly in queue। Never separate।
